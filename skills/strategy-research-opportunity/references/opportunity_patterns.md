# Opportunity Patterns

Use these patterns to convert research signals into distinctive opportunity ideas. The goal is to find a sharp wedge, not a broad category label.

## Signal-to-Idea Translation

| Signal | Ask | Opportunity Pattern |
|--------|-----|---------------------|
| Repeated forum complaints | What workaround do users repeat? | Workflow tool, integration, diagnostic, template, managed service |
| New open-source capability | What previously expensive task becomes cheap? | Vertical automation, local-first app, developer tool, edge inference product |
| Hardware cost/capability shift | What monitoring/control task can now be deployed cheaply? | Low-cost sensor network, managed device, compliance evidence, predictive maintenance |
| Funding cluster | What adjacent or neglected segment is not served by funded companies? | Downmarket wedge, vertical niche, implementation layer, integration layer |
| Regulation or reporting change | Who suddenly has new work and budget? | Compliance workflow, evidence capture, reporting automation, audit trail |
| Job posting trend | What manual role is growing? | Copilot, internal tool, workflow replacement, data collection automation |
| Bad reviews of incumbents | What do customers hate but still pay for? | Simpler product, niche-specific alternative, migration tool, service-plus-software |
| Platform launch | What complements the platform or fills its gaps? | Plugin, analytics, monitoring, provisioning, security, QA, migration |
| Hobbyist project momentum | What commercial buyer has a similar job with budget? | Productized kit, managed service, fleet dashboard, specialized SaaS |

## Distinctive Software Opportunity Shapes

### Vertical workflow wedge
A narrow workflow in a specific industry where users currently stitch together spreadsheets, email, PDFs, and legacy tools.

Strong when:
- Buyer owns a recurring process.
- Mistakes are costly.
- Data can compound over time.
- Incumbents are too broad or too expensive.

### AI-native back-office automation
A workflow where AI can draft, classify, reconcile, monitor, or escalate, but humans still approve high-risk actions.

Strong when:
- Inputs are semi-structured.
- The task is frequent and labor-intensive.
- There is a clear audit trail.
- Accuracy can be measured.

### Developer or operator tooling
A tool that removes friction from building, deploying, monitoring, securing, documenting, or testing software and embedded systems.

Strong when:
- Pain appears in GitHub issues, forums, and Stack Overflow.
- Users already build scripts or internal tools.
- The buyer can adopt bottom-up.

### Data product or benchmark
A recurring information need where public, fragmented, or sensor-derived data can be collected, normalized, and turned into decisions.

Strong when:
- Data is hard to aggregate.
- Decisions are high-value.
- Customers need alerts, rankings, risk scores, or compliance evidence.

### Embedded-software wedge
A low-cost device captures data, controls a process, or proves an event, while the durable business lives in cloud software, analytics, workflow, or managed service.

Strong when:
- Hardware can be simple and cheap.
- Deployment burden is controlled.
- Data has recurring value.
- Customers pay for outcomes, not the board.

### Productized service to SaaS
Start with a done-for-you service where software automation gradually becomes the product.

Strong when:
- Customer pain is urgent.
- Software requirements are unknown.
- Founder can manually deliver value first.
- Repeated delivery reveals a product pattern.

## Embedded / ESP32 Opportunity Patterns

| Pattern | Description | Good Fits | Watchouts |
|---------|-------------|-----------|-----------|
| Retrofit monitoring | Add low-cost sensing to legacy equipment or spaces | Small industrial, agriculture, property, facilities | Installation, calibration, connectivity |
| Compliance evidence capture | Device records temperature, access, motion, energy, water, or process events | Food, healthcare, logistics, rentals, insurance | Audit reliability, data integrity, retention |
| Local-first automation | ESP32 controls or monitors without cloud dependence | Smart home, small business, privacy-sensitive workflows | UX, support, integrations |
| Fleet provisioning layer | Software to deploy and manage many low-cost devices | Makers-to-commercial teams, agencies, IoT integrators | Crowded IoT platforms, support burden |
| Edge inference sensor | On-device detection reduces bandwidth or protects privacy | Audio, vibration, image, environmental signals | Model accuracy, power, update strategy |
| Alerting and maintenance | Device detects anomalies and triggers workflow | Pumps, HVAC, refrigeration, generators, small manufacturing | False positives, field service economics |

## Anti-Generic Filters

Reject or rewrite concepts that have these problems:

- Buyer is "everyone," "small businesses," or "developers" without a narrower segment.
- Solution is just "AI app for [industry]" with no workflow or distribution wedge.
- Idea depends on a market-size claim but has no customer pain evidence.
- Embedded angle is a gadget, not a recurring software or service business.
- Distribution is "run ads" or "post on social" without a reason this audience is reachable.
- The concept copies obvious funded startups without a new segment, model, channel, or technical shift.
- The idea requires complex hardware certification, safety liability, or physical operations but has tiny revenue potential.

## Opportunity Brief Quality Checklist

Each generated opportunity should answer:

1. Who specifically has the problem?
2. What do they do today?
3. What changed that creates the opening now?
4. What is the narrow initial wedge?
5. What is distinctive about the approach?
6. How does software create recurring value?
7. If embedded hardware is involved, why is ESP32/edge the right enabler?
8. What source signals support the idea?
9. What would make the idea false?
10. What next test would quickly validate demand?
