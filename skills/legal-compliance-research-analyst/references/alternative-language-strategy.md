# Alternative Language Strategy

Use this guide to preserve business goals while reducing compliance risk.

## Strategy Framework

| Step | Objective |
|---|---|
| 1. Preserve intent | Keep the core business value proposition |
| 2. Bound the claim | Remove absolute/universal outcome language |
| 3. Add qualifiers | Include necessary conditions and limitations |
| 4. Fit the channel | Adapt wording and disclosure for execution context |
| 5. Validate evidence | Ensure wording scope matches substantiation |

## Common Transformations

| Risky phrasing | Compliant direction |
|---|---|
| Guaranteed outcomes | Designed to support goals, subject to terms and conditions |
| Best/leading without basis | Strong option for defined profiles, based on stated criteria |
| Universal suitability | Appropriate for some customers depending on needs and risk profile |
| Omitted tradeoffs | Include balanced statement of limits, costs, and conditions |

## State-Sensitive Variant Pattern

When state deltas are present, provide:

- Standard copy option (baseline jurisdictions)
- Stricter-state copy option
- Fallback neutral copy for uncertain jurisdictions pending legal determination
