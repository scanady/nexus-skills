# AARP Life Insurance Program — State Compliance Delta Map

**Program:** AARP Life Insurance Program (NYL/AARP)  
**Last Updated:** 2025  
**Purpose:** Documents state-specific requirements that differ from or add to the federal/NAIC baseline. Prioritizes high-volume states and states with known product restrictions.

---

## How to Use This Map

The baseline assumption is NAIC Life Insurance Advertising Model Regulation (#582) compliance, FTC compliance, and NYL company standards. This document captures DELTAS — what each state requires **above and beyond** the baseline.

**Priority tiers:**
- **Tier 1 (Restriction/Action Required):** State has a product restriction or mandatory affirmative obligation
- **Tier 2 (Heightened Standard):** State applies stricter clarity, disclosure, or senior marketing standards
- **Tier 3 (Monitor):** State has standard NAIC adoption with no known material delta; watch for legislative activity

## Tier 1 — Product Restrictions / Mandatory Actions

### New Jersey — CRITICAL
| Issue | Detail | Action Required |
|---|---|---|
| **Guaranteed Acceptance Whole Life not available** | This product is not offered or filed in NJ | All national materials for Guaranteed Acceptance must include "Not available in New Jersey" |
| NJ disclaimer placement | Disclaimer must appear in all advertising for Guaranteed Acceptance where NJ residents may see it | National direct mail, national digital, and any geographically untargeted materials |
| Quote tool / application | NJ-resident applicants must be blocked from the Guaranteed Acceptance application flow at intake | Digital UX / technology team action item |

**NJ Compliance Action:** Before any Guaranteed Acceptance campaign launches, confirm NJ residents are excluded from direct mail lists, suppressed in digital targeting, and blocked in the online application.

---

### New York — HIGH PRIORITY (Domiciliary State)
| Issue | Detail | Action Required |
|---|---|---|
| **Guaranteed Acceptance age band** | Available to members ages 50–75 and eligible spouses ages 45–75 — more restrictive than most states (50–85) | All materials distributed in NY must use the NY age band; national materials must not state "50 to 85" without NY qualification |
| NYDFS advertising file | All life insurance advertising used in NY must be filed with NYDFS; insurer must maintain an advertising file available for examination | Compliance officer maintains file; new campaigns added to file within 30 days of first use |
| NY Insurance Law § 2122 | Prohibition on misrepresentation of terms, benefits, or conditions of any policy in advertising | Standard compliance review applies; NYDFS has active examination program |
| NY Reg 34 (Life Insurance Policy Form Standards) | Policy forms and certificates must be approved by NYDFS before use | Not a marketing requirement per se, but confirms that marketed benefits must match filed/approved certificate terms |
| NY group certificate requirements (§ 4235) | Group certificates must include benefit description, exclusions, and conditions | Compliance with certificate delivery requirements |

## Tier 2 — Heightened State Standards

### California

| Issue | Detail | Standard | Action Required |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Elder Financial Abuse (65+)** | CA Welfare & Institutions Code § 15610 et seq. defines elder financial abuse broadly; aggressive or fear-based marketing to seniors 65+ could trigger elder financial abuse claims under CA law | Heightened FTC-equivalent standard for 65+ audience | Review all CA-targeted materials for fear, urgency, and undue pressure; escalate any concept using health anxiety as primary motivator |
| **Life insurance replacement** | CA Insurance Code § 10509.4 requires replacement notice and disclosure to applicants replacing existing coverage | Replacement notice | If any CA applicant indicates existing coverage, required replacement disclosure must be provided |
| **CCPA (Privacy)** | CA Civil Code § 1798.100 et seq. — consumers have rights to know, delete, and opt out of sale/sharing of personal data; businesses subject to CCPA must maintain compliant privacy notices | Privacy regulation | nylaarp.com must maintain CA-compliant privacy notice; "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" link required; email consent records required |
| **Senior Life Settlement Disclosure** | CA Insurance Code § 10113.1 — insurers must provide senior consumers (age 60+) with a written notice about the potential availability of life settlement contracts as an alternative to lapsing | Disclosure at application/lapse | Applicable at policy issuance and upon any lapse/surrender request from CA resident 60+ |

**CA Compliance Action:** CA-targeted materials should not use fear of death or health decline as primary driver. CCPA compliance is an ongoing technology and policy obligation, not just a creative review concern.

---

### Florida

| Issue | Detail | Standard | Action Required |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Senior Consumer Information (60+)** | FL Statute § 627.9407 — life insurers soliciting seniors (age 60+) must provide specified consumer information addressing consumer rights, complaint process, and contact information for the FL Department of Insurance | Mandatory disclosure | All FL direct mail and call center materials targeting residents 60+ must include or accompany FL senior consumer information pamphlet |
| **Unfair practices — senior exploitation** | FL Statute § 626.9641 — age-based discrimination and deceptive marketing targeting seniors are unfair trade practices; FL DFS has enforcement authority | FTC-equivalent + state AG enforcement | Review all FL materials; no fear-based or urgency tactics |
| **FL DFS complaint history** | FL DFS maintains public complaint data on insurers; complaint trends for the program should be monitored quarterly | Monitor | Pull FL DFS complaint data for NYL/AARP quarterly |

---

### Texas

| Issue | Detail | Standard | Action Required |
|---|---|---|---|
| **TDI Advertising Rules** | TX Insurance Code Chapter 541 prohibits misrepresentation of policy terms, benefits, and conditions | NAIC-equivalent | Standard compliance review |
| **TDI Senior Marketing Guidance** | TDI has issued informal guidance recommending plain language and prohibition on high-pressure tactics for senior consumers; not a formal rule but reflects enforcement posture | Best practice with enforcement risk | TX-targeted materials should use 8th-grade reading level or lower; no high-pressure tactics |
| **Replacement Regulation** | TX Insurance Code Chapter 1114 — replacement notification required if applicant is replacing existing life insurance | Replacement notice | TX applicants indicating existing coverage must receive replacement notice |

---
