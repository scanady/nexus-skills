# AARP Life Insurance Program — Compliant Language Library

**Program:** AARP Life Insurance Program (NYL/AARP)  
**Last Updated:** 2025  
**Purpose:** Provides pre-reviewed language patterns for common claims, benefit descriptions, and disclosures. Use as a starting point — all materials still require compliance review before publication. Do not use language from this library without confirming it matches current certificate terms.

---

## How to Use This Library

- **Green (Approved Pattern):** Language that aligns with regulatory requirements and passes standard review. Use as template.
- **Yellow (Caution — Context-Dependent):** Language that may be compliant depending on context, placement, and adjacent disclosures. Flag for review.
- **Red (Prohibited Pattern):** Language known to create compliance risk. Do not use.

---

## 1. Program Identity & AARP Relationship

### Green — Approved Patterns

| Context | Approved Language |
|---|---|
| Program name (first reference) | "The AARP Life Insurance Program, issued by New York Life Insurance Company" |
| Program name (subsequent reference) | "AARP Life Insurance" or "the Program" |
| Relationship descriptor | "New York Life Insurance Company underwrites life insurance for AARP members through the AARP Life Insurance Program." |
| AARP role | "AARP makes this program available to its members." |
| Royalty disclosure (where required) | "New York Life pays royalties to AARP for use of AARP's intellectual property. These royalties are used for the general purposes of AARP." |
| AARP nonprofit reference | "AARP is a nonprofit organization dedicated to empowering Americans 50 and older." |

### Red — Prohibited Patterns

| Prohibited Language | Reason |
|---|---|
| "AARP-endorsed coverage" | Implies independent endorsement; obscures licensing/royalty relationship |
| "Recommended by AARP" | Same as above |
| "AARP's best life insurance option for seniors" | Superlative endorsement claim; Krukas v. AARP litigation pattern |
| "AARP has chosen New York Life as its exclusive provider" | Exclusivity claim requires legal verification; "chosen" implies independent evaluation |
| "Backed by AARP" | Implies financial guarantee from AARP; AARP is not the underwriter |

## 2. Eligibility Claims

### Green — Approved Patterns

| Context | Approved Language |
|---|---|
| Primary eligibility | "Available to AARP members age 50 and older and their eligible spouses." |
| Spouse eligibility (Term Life) | "Eligible spouses ages 45 to 74 may also apply." |
| Spouse eligibility (Whole Life) | "Eligible spouses ages 45 to 80 may also apply." |
| Spouse eligibility (Guaranteed Acceptance) | "Eligible spouses ages 45 to 85 may also apply." [Note: add NY restriction for NY-targeted materials] |
| Membership requirement | "AARP membership is required. Membership is open to anyone age 18 and older." |
| Anyone can join | "Anyone can join AARP — membership is open to adults of all ages." |

### Yellow — Caution Patterns

| Language | Condition for Use |
|---|---|
| "For people 50 and older" | Only permissible if AARP membership requirement is disclosed in the same placement |
| "Designed for AARP members" | Fine as a framing statement; must not imply eligibility is automatic without membership |

### Red — Prohibited Patterns

| Prohibited Language | Reason |
|---|---|
| "Available to adults age 50 and older" (without membership caveat) | Hides AARP membership requirement |
| "Anyone can get this coverage" | False — membership required |

## 3. No Medical Exam Claims

### Green — Approved Patterns

| Context | Approved Language |
|---|---|
| General | "No medical exam required." |
| Term Life and Whole Life | "Answer a few health questions — no medical exam needed." |
| Guaranteed Acceptance | "No health questions required. Acceptance is guaranteed." [Must be paired with waiting period disclosure — see Section 6] |

### Yellow — Caution Patterns

| Language | Condition for Use |
|---|---|
| "Easy to qualify" | Only for Guaranteed Acceptance with waiting period disclosure present |
| "No doctor visits required" | Accurate but should be positioned carefully to not overstate ease of coverage onset |

### Red — Prohibited Patterns

| Prohibited Language | Reason |
|---|---|
| "No questions asked" (for Term or Whole Life) | These products DO ask health questions; only Guaranteed Acceptance has no health questions |
| "Automatic approval" | No product has automatic approval without at least a membership verification step |

## 4. Benefit Claims — Term Life Insurance

### Green — Approved Patterns

| Context | Approved Language |
|---|---|
| Product description | "Level Benefit Term Life Insurance provides coverage up to age 80 with a death benefit that remains level throughout the term." |
| Premium structure | "Your premiums will increase when you enter each new 5-year age band." |
| Initial rate framing | "Your initial monthly rate is $[X], with rates increasing as you age." |
| Coverage range | "Choose coverage from $10,000 up to $150,000. Higher coverage amounts may be available by calling [number]." |
| Conversion right | "You can convert your term policy to permanent life insurance at or before age 80." |
| AutoPay discount | "Choose AutoPay and save up to $12 per year on your premiums." |

### Red — Prohibited Patterns

| Prohibited Language | Reason |
|---|---|
| "Lock in your rate" (without disclosure) | Rates are NOT locked in — they increase every 5 years |
| "Level premiums" for Term Life | Term premiums are NOT level — this is the description for Whole Life |
| "Lifetime coverage" for Term Life | Term expires at age 80; it is not lifetime coverage |
| "Protect your family for life" (for Term, without clarification) | Implies permanent coverage; must clarify "until age 80" |

## 5. Benefit Claims — Whole Life Insurance

### Green — Approved Patterns

| Context | Approved Language |
|---|---|
| Product description | "Whole Life Insurance provides permanent, lifelong coverage with premiums that never increase." |
| Level premium | "Your monthly premium stays the same — it never goes up." |
| Coverage range | "Choose coverage from $[X] up to $100,000 online. Higher amounts may be available by calling [number]." |
| Paid-up status | "Once your policy is paid up, typically around age 95, you no longer need to make premium payments — and your coverage continues." |
| Permanence | "Your coverage lasts your entire lifetime — it never expires." |

### Yellow — Caution Patterns

| Language | Condition for Use |
|---|---|
| "Build a legacy" | Fine as positioning language; must not imply a savings or investment component |
| "Leave something behind for your family" | Permissible; ensure coverage amount context is present |

### Red — Prohibited Patterns

| Prohibited Language | Reason |
|---|---|
| "Whole life insurance grows in value" (without cash value qualification) | This product description must be verified against certificate; do not imply investment growth unless the product has a cash value feature |
| "Your money back if you cancel" | Not accurate unless surrender value language is in the certificate — verify before using |

## 6. Benefit Claims — Guaranteed Acceptance Whole Life

### Green — Approved Patterns

| Context | Approved Language |
|---|---|
| Product description | "Guaranteed Acceptance Whole Life Insurance is available to AARP members ages 50 to 85. No health questions are required — your acceptance is guaranteed." |
| Waiting period disclosure — SHORT form | "This policy has a waiting period. Benefits paid in the first 2 years for death by natural causes will equal 110% of premiums paid." |
| Waiting period disclosure — LONG form | "Please note: This policy includes a graded benefit period. If the insured dies of natural causes during the first 2 years of the policy, the benefit paid to your beneficiaries will equal 110% of all premiums you have paid, not the full death benefit amount. Full benefits are payable for death due to an accident at any time." |
| NJ disclaimer | "Not available in New Jersey." |
| NY age band (NY-targeted materials only) | "Available to AARP members and their eligible spouses ages 50 to 75 in New York." |
| Coverage range | "Choose coverage from $[X] up to $30,000. Higher amounts may be available by calling [number]." |
| Accidental death benefit | "If death occurs as a result of an accident, the full benefit is payable from day one." |

### Red — Prohibited Patterns

| Prohibited Language | Reason |
|---|---|
| "Full coverage from day one" | False — graded benefit applies in first 2 years for natural causes |
| "No waiting period" | False — 2-year waiting period exists |
| "Your family gets $[X] immediately" | Implies immediate full payout; waiting period may apply |
| "Guaranteed payout of $[X]" (without waiting period qualification) | Misleading — waiting period creates condition on payout |
| "Available in all states" | False — not available in NJ |

## 7. Benefit Claims — Living Benefits (Accelerated Death Benefit)

### Green — Approved Patterns

| Context | Approved Language |
|---|---|
| Feature description | "This policy includes a Living Benefit feature. If you are diagnosed with a qualifying illness, you may be able to access up to 50% of your policy's death benefit while you are still living." |
| Qualifying illness trigger | "The Living Benefit is available if you are diagnosed with a qualifying terminal, chronic, or critical illness, as defined in your policy." |
| State availability disclaimer | "Living Benefit feature is not available in all states." |
| Benefit limit | "You may access up to half your policy's face amount as a living benefit." |

### Red — Prohibited Patterns

| Prohibited Language | Reason |
|---|---|
| "Access your benefit anytime" | False — qualifying illness required |
| "Use your life insurance while you're alive" (standalone, without qualifier) | Must be paired with qualifying illness requirement |
| "Cash out your policy" | Incorrect framing; this is not a cash value or surrender |
| "Living benefits available to all policyholders" | Not available in all states |

## 8. Urgency and Enrollment Window Claims

### Green — Approved Patterns

| Context | Approved Language |
|---|---|
| Genuine enrollment deadline | "This special enrollment period ends [date]." [Only if a genuine, verifiable deadline exists] |
| Age-based urgency (factual) | "The younger you enroll, the lower your initial rate." |
| Open enrollment (when applicable) | "Enroll now — no medical exam required." |

### Yellow — Caution Patterns

| Language | Condition for Use |
|---|---|
| "Act before rates change" | Only if a rate change is genuinely scheduled; must be verified with actuarial/product team before use |
| "Limited time offer" | Only if an actual time limit exists; must include end date |

### Red — Prohibited Patterns

| Prohibited Language | Reason |
|---|---|
| "You may lose your eligibility soon" (without factual basis) | Manufactured urgency; exploits health anxiety |
| "This offer may not be available after [arbitrary date]" | False urgency unless tied to genuine business rule |
| "Act now before it's too late" | Fear-based urgency; violates AARP anti-ageism standards and FTC senior marketing guidance |
| "Your age is working against you — don't wait" | Exploits aging anxiety; prohibited by AARP brand standards |
| Countdown timers (web/email) without genuine expiration | Creates false urgency |

## 9. Financial Strength Claims

### Green — Approved Patterns

| Context | Approved Language |
|---|---|
| AM Best citation | "New York Life Insurance Company has received the highest financial strength rating awarded to any life insurer by A.M. Best (A++, Superior, as of [year]). This rating is subject to change." |
| Abbreviated reference | "New York Life has received A.M. Best's highest rating (A++, Superior, as of [year])." |
| J.D. Power reference | "New York Life ranked #8 out of 22 companies in J.D. Power's 2025 U.S. Individual Life Insurance Study for customer satisfaction." |

### Red — Prohibited Patterns

| Prohibited Language | Reason |
|---|---|
| "#1 in customer satisfaction" | Not supported by J.D. Power ranking (ranked #8) |
| "Best-rated life insurance company" (unqualified) | Superlative must be substantiated by a specific, cited source |
| AM Best rating without source and date | Required elements for substantiated financial strength claim |

## 10. Standard Disclosure Blocks

### Short Form (Space-Constrained Placements — Digital Ads, Social)
> *"The AARP Life Insurance Program, issued by New York Life Insurance Company, 51 Madison Avenue, New York, NY. Not available in all states. Premiums increase with age. Guaranteed Acceptance policies have a waiting period. AARP membership required."*

### Long Form (Direct Mail, Landing Pages)
> *"The AARP Life Insurance Program is issued by New York Life Insurance Company, 51 Madison Avenue, New York, NY 10010. New York Life pays royalties to AARP for use of its intellectual property. AARP membership is required to purchase. Not available in all states or for all ages. Level Benefit Term Life Insurance premiums increase when you enter a new 5-year age band. Guaranteed Acceptance Whole Life Insurance has a waiting period: if the insured dies of natural causes in the first 2 policy years, the benefit paid to your beneficiaries is 110% of premiums paid. Guaranteed Acceptance Whole Life is not available in NJ. In New York, Guaranteed Acceptance Whole Life is available to members and eligible spouses ages 50–75. Living Benefit feature is not available in all states. This is a solicitation for insurance."*

### Replacement Notice (Where Replacement Disclosure is Required by State)
> *"If you are replacing existing life insurance coverage, you have the right to review your current policy before deciding to replace it. If you decide to replace your existing policy, you should terminate it only after the new policy has been issued and you have decided to accept it."*
