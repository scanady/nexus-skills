## Structure

1. **Hook** — First line grabs or gets skipped
2. **Context** — Why this matters to them right now
3. **Value** — Useful content, story, proof, or insight
4. **CTA** — One clear next action
5. **Sign-off** — Human, warm, brief

---

## Formatting

- Short paragraphs (1–3 sentences). White space between every block
- Bullet points for scanability
- Bold for emphasis — use sparingly
- Mobile-first — most email is read on phone. Short lines win

---

## Tone

- Conversational, not formal
- First-person (I/we) and second-person (you)
- Active voice throughout
- Match brand voice but lean friendly
- Read it aloud — sounds human? Ship it

---

## Length

- 50–125 words → transactional (billing, confirmations, nudges)
- 150–300 words → educational (features, tips, how-to)
- 300–500 words → story-driven (case studies, origin stories)
- If it's long, it better be good

---

## CTA Buttons vs. Links

- **Buttons** → primary actions, high-visibility placement
- **Links** → secondary actions, in-text references
- One primary CTA per email — never two competing actions
- Button text: Action + outcome ("Start your trial" not "Click here")

## Personalization

### Merge Fields
- First name — fallback to "there" or "friend" if blank, never empty
- Company name (B2B)
- Relevant data: usage stats, plan tier, last activity

### Dynamic Content
- By segment (trial vs. paid, new vs. long-term)
- By behavior (engaged vs. inactive, feature user vs. non-user)
- By profile (industry, role, use case in B2B)

### Triggered Sends
- Action-based > time-based — triggered emails are more relevant
- Examples: feature used → milestone hit → inactivity → usage limit reached
- Use behavioral triggers over calendar delays when data allows

---

## Segmentation Strategies

### By Behavior
- Openers vs. non-openers
- Clickers vs. non-clickers
- Active vs. inactive

### By Stage
- Trial vs. paid
- New vs. long-term
- Engaged vs. at-risk

### By Profile (B2B)
- Industry / vertical
- Role / persona
- Company size or use case

## Testing and Optimization

### What to Test (one variable at a time)
- Subject lines — highest impact on open rate
- Send time and day
- Email length
- CTA copy and placement
- Personalization level
- Sequence timing and delay

### How to Test
- A/B test one variable at a time
- Wait for sufficient sample size before calling winner
- Document learnings — compound over time

### Metrics to Track

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Open rate | 20–40% |
| Click rate | 2–5% |
| Unsubscribe rate | < 0.5% |
| Conversion rate | sequence-goal-specific |
| Revenue per email | track if monetization is goal |
