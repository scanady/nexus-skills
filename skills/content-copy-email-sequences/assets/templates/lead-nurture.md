# Lead Nurture Sequence (Pre-Sale)

**Trigger**: Lead magnet downloaded, webinar attended, or contact added to pre-sale list
**Goal**: Build trust, demonstrate expertise, convert to customer
**Length**: 6–8 emails over 2–3 weeks
**Exit conditions**: Converts to customer · requests demo or sales call · unsubscribes

---

## Sequence Blueprint

```
Sequence Name:
Trigger: Lead magnet downloaded / pre-sale list entry

Timing: Immediate → Day 2–3 → Day 4–5 → Day 6–8 → Day 9–11 → Day 12–14 → Day 15–18 → Day 19–21
Exit conditions: Converts, books demo, unsubscribes
Segmentation rules: [by lead source or topic interest if applicable]
```

---

## Email-by-Email Template

**Email 1: Deliver + Introduce (Immediate)**
- Deliver the lead magnet
- Brief intro to who you are and what you do
- Preview of what's coming next — set expectations

**Email 2: Expand on Topic (Day 2–3)**
- Related insight building on what the lead magnet covered
- Establish expertise without pitching
- Light CTA to relevant content (article, video, resource)

**Email 3: Problem Deep-Dive (Day 4–5)**
- Articulate their problem deeply — show you understand
- Empathize; don't pitch
- Hint at a solution framework to come

**Email 4: Solution Framework (Day 6–8)**
- Your methodology or approach
- Educational, not salesy — builds naturally toward your product
- Positions you as the obvious guide

**Email 5: Case Study (Day 9–11)**
- Real results from a real customer
- Specific and relatable to this audience
- Soft CTA to learn more or explore

**Email 6: Differentiation (Day 12–14)**
- Why your approach is different from alternatives
- Address how you compare without disparaging competitors
- Build preference before the ask

**Email 7: Objection Handler (Day 15–18)**
- Address the #1 objection head-on
- FAQ or myth-busting format works well
- Reduce friction to decide

**Email 8: Direct Offer (Day 19–21)**
- Clear pitch with strong value proposition
- Specific CTA (start trial, book demo, purchase)
- Urgency or incentive if available

---

## Segmentation

- **By lead source**: Different lead magnets may need different topic angles in Email 2–3
- **By engagement**: High openers/clickers → accelerate to Email 7–8 sooner
- **By role/industry** (B2B): Customize case study (Email 5) to match their vertical

---

## Copy Rules

- Emails 1–4: zero selling — pure value. Earn the right to pitch
- Email 5: soft CTA only — case study, not an offer
- Email 8: the only email where a direct ask is appropriate
- Tone: expert guide, not salesperson. First-person works well

---

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Open rate | 25–40% |
| Click rate | 3–6% |
| Demo/trial conversion | depends on offer + sequence length |
| Unsubscribe rate | < 0.5% |

---

## Audit Checklist

- [ ] Email 1 delivers lead magnet in full
- [ ] Emails 1–4 contain no direct product pitch
- [ ] Problem deep-dive (Email 3) resonates with the specific audience
- [ ] Case study (Email 5) is specific with real numbers or outcomes
- [ ] Direct offer (Email 8) includes clear CTA, value prop, and urgency if available
- [ ] Exit condition removes converters from sequence
- [ ] Subject lines are benefit- or curiosity-driven, 40–60 chars
- [ ] Sequence timing is adjusted for sales cycle length (longer cycle = slower cadence)
