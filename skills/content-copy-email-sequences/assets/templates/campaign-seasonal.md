# Campaign / Seasonal

**Trigger**: Calendar event, promotional period, product launch, or pricing change
**Goal**: Drive time-bound conversions or communicate important updates
**Length**: 2–4 emails depending on campaign duration
**Exit conditions**: Purchases / converts · campaign end date passes · unsubscribes

---

## Sequence Blueprint

```
Sequence Name:
Trigger: [campaign launch date / event]
Goal: [conversion target or communication goal]
Length: 2–4 emails
Timing: Day 0 (announcement) → midway (reminder) → final hours/day (last chance)
Exit conditions: Converts, campaign period ends, unsubscribes
Segmentation rules: [existing customers vs. prospects vs. lapsed — different offers may apply]
```

---

## Campaign Variants

### Promotional / Seasonal

Common triggers: Black Friday, Cyber Monday, New Year, end-of-fiscal year, back-to-work, industry-specific seasons

| Phase | Timing | Purpose |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Announcement | Day 0 | Reveal the offer clearly |
| Reminder | Midway | Keep it front of mind |
| Last chance | Final hours or day | Urgency close |

**Copy rules**:
- Announcement: lead with the offer — don't bury the lede
- Reminder: add a reason to act now (social proof, counter, benefit recap)
- Last chance: urgency must be real — don't fake it or use it every campaign

---

### Product Update

**Trigger**: New feature release or significant product improvement
**Format**: Single email per major release (or 2-email for large launches)

**Include**:
- What's new — clear, simple, benefit-led (not just "we added X")
- Why it matters — what does the user get or save?
- How to use it — direct in-product link
- Who asked for it — community acknowledgment if applicable

**Segmentation**: Target users who'd benefit most, or who requested the feature. Power users first for beta-feel.

---

### Pricing Update

**Trigger**: Price change announcement
**Format**: 3-part sequence (major changes) or single email (minor)

| Phase | Timing | Purpose |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Announcement | 30–60 days before | Full transparency, explain why |
| Reminder | 14 days before | Options recap (lock in, upgrade, downgrade) |
| Final notice | 7 days before | Last chance to act at old price |

**Copy rules**:
- Honest and direct — explain the reason (value delivered, costs, etc.)
- Offer options: annual lock-in, grandfathering if possible, downgrade path
- Advance notice builds trust even when the news isn't good

---

### Monthly Newsletter / Roundup

**Trigger**: Time-based (monthly)
**Format**: Single recurring email

**Content mix**: Product updates + tips · customer stories · educational content · company news · industry insights

**Best practices**:
- Consistent send day and time
- Scannable format — headers and bullets
- One primary CTA per issue
- Unsubscribes are acceptable — a clean, engaged list is the goal

---

## Segmentation

- **Existing customers vs. prospects**: Different offers, different urgency levels, different context
- **By engagement tier**: High-engage gets early access or exclusive preview; low-engage gets standard
- **Lapsed customers**: Seasonal campaigns are a natural re-engagement opportunity — consider adding a win-back angle

---

## Copy Rules

- Urgency must be real — don't manufacture false countdowns
- Offers should be relevant to the segment — don't spam your whole list with an offer only some can use
- Pricing updates: be transparent. Customers forgive price increases; they don't forgive surprises.
- Product updates: benefit-led, not feature-led. "You can now do X" not "We built Y"

---

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Open rate (announcement) | 25–40% |
| Conversion rate | depends on offer + segment |
| Unsubscribe rate | < 0.5% (spike is acceptable on pricing announcements) |
| Revenue per email sent | track for promotional campaigns |

---

## Audit Checklist

- [ ] Campaign type identified (promotional / product update / pricing / newsletter)
- [ ] Audience segmented appropriately — not a full-list blast unless genuinely relevant to all
- [ ] Urgency in "last chance" email is real, not manufactured
- [ ] Pricing update sends 30–60 days in advance, not last minute
- [ ] Product update email is benefit-led, not feature-announcement copy
- [ ] Announcement email leads with the offer/news — no buried lede
- [ ] Exit condition removes converters from the campaign burst
- [ ] Newsletter has consistent send cadence (don't skip months or double up)
