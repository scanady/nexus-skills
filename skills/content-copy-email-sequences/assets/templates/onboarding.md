# Onboarding Sequence (Product Users)

**Trigger**: User signs up (free or trial) and enters the product
**Goal**: Drive to "aha moment", activate key features, reduce early churn
**Length**: 5–7 emails over 14 days
**Exit conditions**: Completes activation milestone · converts to paid · trial expires → hand off to win-back

---

## Sequence Blueprint

```
Sequence Name:
Trigger: User account created / first login
Goal: [activation action — define the aha moment]
Length: 5–7 emails
Timing: Immediate → Day 1 → Day 2–3 → Day 4–5 → Day 7 → Day 10–12 → Day 14+
Exit conditions: Completes activation milestone, converts to paid, trial expires
Segmentation rules: [split by engagement level — active users vs. stuck/inactive]
```

---

## Email-by-Email Template

**Email 1: Welcome + First Step (Immediate)**
- Confirm signup — clear and reassuring
- One critical action with a direct in-app link (not a list of tasks)
- Don't overwhelm — single focus only

**Email 2: Getting Started Help (Day 1)**
- If step 1 not completed: friendly, low-pressure nudge
- Quick tip or short video
- Surface the support option (docs, chat, human)

**Email 3: Feature Highlight (Day 2–3)**
- Key feature they should know about now
- Specific use case — not abstract
- Deep link directly into the product to try it

**Email 4: Success Story (Day 4–5)**
- Customer who succeeded at a similar stage
- Relatable journey and concrete outcome
- Motivational — not promotional

**Email 5: Check-In (Day 7)**
- Genuine "how's it going?" — not a feature pitch
- Ask for feedback or surface blockers
- Offer help (support, demo, call)

**Email 6: Advanced Tip (Day 10–12)**
- Power feature or workflow for more advanced use
- Target engaged users — suppress or soften for inactive
- Level-up content that rewards exploration

**Email 7: Upgrade / Expand (Day 14+)**
- Trial users: conversion push with value summary
- Free users: upgrade prompt
- Paid users: expansion or higher-tier opportunity

---

## Segmentation

- **Active vs. inactive by Day 3**: Active users → advance. Inactive → re-send Email 2 nudge or branch to check-in
- **By trial type**: Free trial vs. freemium vs. new paid → Email 7 CTA differs
- **By role** (B2B teams): Individual user vs. admin → different feature highlights and CTAs

---

## Copy Rules

- Email coordinates with in-app onboarding — email supports, does not duplicate what the product already shows
- Never send an email reminding them to do something they already did
- Each email should have an in-app deep link, not just "log in and look around"
- Tone: helpful guide, not marketing blast

---

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Activation rate (key action completion) | varies by product — define your aha moment |
| Email 1 open rate | 50%+ |
| Feature adoption from email | track via UTM / in-app events |
| Trial-to-paid conversion | baseline depends on pricing model |

---

## Audit Checklist

- [ ] Aha moment / activation action defined before writing sequence
- [ ] Email 1 fires immediately on signup with single critical action
- [ ] Each email has deep link into product (not just homepage)
- [ ] Email sequence does not duplicate in-app onboarding copy
- [ ] Inactive users identified by Day 3 and branched appropriately
- [ ] Email 6 (advanced tip) suppressed or softened for non-engaged users
- [ ] Email 7 variant exists for trial-to-paid conversion
- [ ] Exit condition removes users who complete activation from the sequence
