# Trial Win-Back

**Trigger**: Trial or free period expires without conversion
**Goal**: Convert expired trial users to paid, or gather feedback for product improvement
**Length**: 3–4 emails over 30 days
**Exit conditions**: Converts to paid · declines explicitly · no response at Day 30 → suppress

---

## Sequence Blueprint

```
Sequence Name:
Trigger: Trial expiry date
Goal: [paid conversion or meaningful feedback]
Length: 3–4 emails
Timing: Day 1 → Day 7 → Day 14 → Day 30
Exit conditions: Converts to paid, explicitly declines, or no response at Day 30
Segmentation rules: [by trial engagement level — critical to personalize]
```

---

## Email-by-Email Template

**Email 1: What You're Missing (Day 1 Post-Expiry)**
- Subject: "Your trial ended — here's what happens next"
- Neutral, not panicky
- Remind what they had access to (specific features, not generic)
- Clear CTA to convert — low friction, direct link

**Email 2: Feedback Request (Day 7)**
- Subject: "What held you back, [Name]?"
- Genuine ask — what didn't work, what was unclear
- 1–2 question max, or link to short survey
- Optional: surface a specific objection and address it if known

**Email 3: Incentive (Day 14)**
- Subject: "One more thing before you go"
- Offer if appropriate: discount, extended trial, reduced plan
- Frame as a genuine second chance, not desperation
- Clear expiry on the offer

**Email 4: Final Reach-Out (Day 30)**
- Subject: "Leaving the door open"
- Low pressure, no ask
- Brief note: offer stands, door is open whenever they're ready
- Ends the sequence without burning the relationship

---

## Segmentation by Trial Engagement

**High engagement** (used product regularly, reached key features):
- Friction is the problem, not fit
- Email 1: emphasize what they'll lose access to, CTA to convert immediately
- Email 3: discount or extended trial removes the barrier
- Skip heavy feedback ask — they know the product

**Low engagement** (logged in rarely, didn't complete setup):
- Value wasn't clear or setup was a barrier
- Email 1: lighter tone, offer fresh start or guided setup
- Email 2: feedback is critical — understand why they didn't engage
- Email 3: offer extended trial with onboarding help, not just discount

**Zero engagement** (never logged in or barely tried):
- Fit or timing problem
- Email 1: ask what happened — genuine curiosity
- Email 2: offer demo or 1:1 walkthrough
- Skip incentive offer until engagement intent is shown

---

## Copy Rules

- Email 1–2: no desperation. Neutral to curious tone.
- Email 3: incentive should feel earned, not cheap
- Email 4: leave the relationship intact — they may come back later
- Never imply they made a mistake by not converting

---

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Win-back conversion rate | 5–15% depending on trial length and product |
| Feedback response rate (Email 2) | 10–20% |
| Day 14 incentive redemption rate | benchmark per offer type |
| Long-term win-back (Day 30+) | track separately — they often convert months later |

---

## Audit Checklist

- [ ] Sequence segments by trial engagement level before Email 1 sends
- [ ] Email 1 fires Day 1 post-expiry (not Day 0 — give one day of grace)
- [ ] High-engagement users get direct conversion path, not heavy feedback ask
- [ ] Feedback email (Email 2) asks 1–2 questions maximum
- [ ] Incentive offer (Email 3) has a clear expiry date
- [ ] Email 4 is genuinely low-pressure — no hidden ask
- [ ] Converters exit sequence immediately
- [ ] Non-responders at Day 30 suppressed from trial win-back (can re-enter future campaigns)
