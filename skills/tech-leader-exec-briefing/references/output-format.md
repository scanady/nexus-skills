# Output Format

```markdown
# Executive Briefing: [Topic Title]

**To:** [Audience — e.g., Chief Executive Officer]
**From:** [CIO / CTO]
**Date:** [Current Date]
**Purpose:** [Inform / Decision Required / Approval Requested / Escalation]

---

## Summary

[2–4 sentences. Situation, business significance, what's being asked. Reader who stops here must understand essential message.]

---

## Context

[2–3 paragraphs. Background exec needs — not technical history, but business context. What changed, what created this situation, why it matters now. Include scale, scope, or financial context.]

---

## Business Implications

[2–3 paragraphs. What this means for business. Risk, cost, competitive position, customer impact, regulatory exposure, operational capability. Be specific. Quantify where possible.]

---

## Options Considered
*(Only when decision required)*

**Option 1: [Name]**
[2–3 sentences. Option, benefits, drawbacks in business terms.]

**Option 2: [Name]**
[2–3 sentences. Option, benefits, drawbacks in business terms.]

**Option 3: [Name]** *(if applicable)*
[2–3 sentences.]

---

## Recommendation

[1–2 paragraphs. Recommended path, clearly and directly. Reasoning in business terms. No hedging. If risk exists, name it and explain why recommendation still right.]

---

## Decision / Action Required

[1 short paragraph or brief list. What decision, approval, or action requested? By when? What happens if no decision made?]

*(Omit if briefing purely informational.)*
```

---

## Variants

**Short form ("One-Pager"):**
- Summary (3–4 sentences)
- Business Implications (1 paragraph)
- Recommendation + Action Required (1 paragraph)

**Expanded form:**
Expand Context and Business Implications sections. Add **Supporting Detail** appendix for data, timelines, or technical notes execs may want on demand but shouldn't be forced to read.
