# Output Format

```markdown
# Technology Vision: [Title — memorable, specific descriptor]

**Author:** [CIO / CTO]
**Date:** [Current Date]
**Horizon:** [e.g., 2026–2029 / 3-Year / 5-Year]

---

## The Vision

[Anchor statement — 1–2 sentences. Essence of entire vision. Specific, bold, clear.]

---

## Where We Stand

[2–3 paragraphs. Honest assessment of current tech landscape. What works, what doesn't, what holds org back. Name gap. Don't dwell — establish starting point and move forward.]

---

## The Forces Shaping Our Direction

[1–2 paragraphs. External and internal forces making this necessary and urgent — competitive dynamics, market shifts, tech evolution, customer expectations, regulatory changes, business strategy pivots. Answers "why now" and "why this direction."]

---

## [Strategic Theme 1: Descriptive Title]

[2–3 paragraphs. What theme means, what it changes, business capability enabled, what org looks like when realized. Name outcomes, not activities.]

---

## [Strategic Theme 2: Descriptive Title]

[2–3 paragraphs. Same structure. Show how it builds on or complements prior theme.]

---

## [Strategic Theme 3: Descriptive Title]

[2–3 paragraphs.]

---

## [Strategic Theme 4: Descriptive Title] *(if applicable)*

[2–3 paragraphs.]

---

## What We Are Choosing Not to Do

[1–2 paragraphs. Deliberate exclusions — tech not adopted, investments not made, directions not taken. Frame as disciplined strategic choices strengthening focus. Builds credibility.]

---

## The Organization We Are Building

[1–2 paragraphs. People, culture, operating model. What kind of tech org needed? What changes in talent, skills, structure, ways of working? Statement about team and culture, not org chart.]

---

## What This Means for the Business

[1–2 paragraphs. Payoff. What can business do that it can't today? How is competitive position changed? Connect tech vision to business transformation in CEO/board terms.]

---

## The Path Forward

[1–2 paragraphs. Closing. Reaffirm direction with conviction. If endorsement or investment needed, state directly. End with forward energy.]
```

---

## Variants

**Anchor Statement Only:**
Produce 1–2 sentence anchor statement with 3–4 alternative phrasings.

**Vision on a Page:**
- Vision (anchor statement)
- Where We Stand (3–4 sentences)
- Strategic Themes (1 paragraph each, titled)
- What This Means for Business (1 paragraph)

**Internal Team Version:**
Aspirational, operationally connecting tone — "This is what we're building and why your work matters." Add "How This Shapes Our Priorities" section bridging vision to near-term execution.
