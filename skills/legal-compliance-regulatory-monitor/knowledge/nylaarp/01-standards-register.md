# AARP Life Insurance Program — Compliance Standards Register

**Program:** AARP Life Insurance Program (NYL/AARP)  
**Last Updated:** 2025  
**Schema:** Company → Industry → Federal → State (4-layer model)

---

## How to Use This Register

Each control entry contains:
- **Control ID** — unique identifier for cross-referencing
- **Layer** — Company / Industry / Federal / State
- **Control Statement** — the binding requirement
- **Applicability** — which products/channels are in scope
- **Evidence Required** — what documentation demonstrates compliance
- **Disclosure Required** — mandated language or notice
- **Risk Level** — HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW based on enforcement history

## Layer 2 — Industry Standards (NAIC Model Regulations)

### NAIC Life Insurance Advertising Model Regulation (#582)

> Adopted in whole or substantially in most states. Governs all advertising of individual and group life insurance.

| Control ID | Control Statement | Applicability | Evidence Required | Risk Level |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| IND-01 | Advertising must not use words, phrases, or illustrations that are misleading or deceptive as to the nature, terms, conditions, benefits, or costs of the policy | All products, all channels | Line-by-line review against current certificate terms | HIGH |
| IND-02 | Advertising that uses the words "free," "no cost," "at no extra cost," or similar phrases is prohibited unless the benefit is genuinely free | All products | No "free" framing for member benefits; AARP membership cost must not be obscured if mentioned | MEDIUM |
| IND-03 | Advertising must not imply the company has a "special" relationship with a regulatory body or government | All materials | Prohibition on government affiliation claims; AARP's nonprofit status must not imply government backing | HIGH |
| IND-04 | Any policy illustrated must be identified by its correct generic name (e.g., "term life insurance," "whole life insurance," "guaranteed issue whole life") | All materials | Generic product name present in all advertising |MEDIUM |
| IND-05 | Advertising must not emphasize a low initial premium without disclosing that premiums may increase | Term Life (5-year escalating premiums) | Premium increase disclosure present; not buried in fine print | HIGH |
| IND-06 | Advertising for a policy with a graded or limited benefit during a waiting period must disclose that limitation clearly and prominently | Guaranteed Acceptance Whole Life | Graded benefit disclosure present; minimum font size and placement rules per state | HIGH |
| IND-07 | Testimonials or endorsements must represent the actual experience of the endorser and may not be presented out of context | Any testimonial materials | Testimonial accuracy documentation; AARP member testimonials must be bona fide | MEDIUM |
| IND-08 | Comparison of one insurer's policy to another's must be factual, fair, and complete | Any comparison materials | Fact-check record; comparison must not cherry-pick favorable attributes | MEDIUM |

## Layer 3 — Federal Standards

### FTC Act Section 5 — Unfair or Deceptive Acts or Practices (UDAP)

| Control ID | Control Statement | Applicability | Evidence Required | Risk Level |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| FED-01 | Advertising may not contain material misrepresentations likely to mislead a reasonable consumer | All products, all channels | Reasonable consumer standard review; claim substantiation on file | HIGH |
| FED-02 | Material facts that consumers would rely on in making a purchase decision must be disclosed clearly and conspicuously | All products — especially graded benefit, premium increases, state restrictions | Disclosure prominence review; not in fine print below a visual threshold | HIGH |
| FED-03 | Endorsement or testimonial advertising must clearly disclose any material connection between the endorser and advertiser | AARP endorsement framing | AARP royalty relationship disclosure; "AARP receives compensation" language or equivalent | HIGH |
| FED-04 | Online disclosures must meet "clear and conspicuous" standard — proximity, prominence, and accessibility on all device types | nylaarp.com; AARP digital ads; email | Digital disclosure audit; mobile display review | MEDIUM |
