# IV. A Few Matters of Form

Conventions for headings, numerals, parentheses, quotations, and titles.

---

### Headings

Leave a blank line after a heading before body text begins. Heading text should be a noun phrase or imperative — not a complete sentence with terminal punctuation.

---

### Numerals

Write dates and serial numbers in figures, not words.

> April 6, 2024 — not "the sixth of April"  
> Chapter 3, Step 2, Rule 7 — not "chapter three"  
> 352nd Infantry — not "three hundred fifty-second"

---

### Parentheses

Punctuate the sentence outside the parentheses as if the parenthetical material weren't there. Punctuate inside only if the enclosed material is a complete standalone sentence.

> He ran the migration (his third attempt), but it failed.  
> He ran the migration. (No rollback plan existed.)

---

### Quotations

| Situation | Form |
|-----------|------|
| Formal citation or documentary evidence | Colon + quotation marks |
| Direct object or apposition | Comma + quotation marks |
| Clause introduced by *that* | No quotation marks (indirect discourse) |
| Proverbial phrases, common idioms | No quotation marks |

> The spec states: "All tokens expire after 24 hours."  
> The error reads, "connection refused."  
> The docs note that all tokens expire after 24 hours.  
> The build is on fire.

For multi-line code or quoted blocks, use a code fence or blockquote. Don't use quotation marks around multi-line blocks.

---

### Titles

Italicize titles of books, publications, and long works. Capitalize significant words.

> *The Elements of Style*, *Clean Code*, *RFC 9110*

For short works (articles, chapters, episodes), use quotation marks instead of italics.

---

### Syllabication (Word Breaks)

When breaking a word across a line:
- Break at the word's natural formation boundary: *know-ledge*, not *knowl-edge*
- Break on a vowel: *edi-ble*, *ordi-nary*
- Break between doubled consonants: *Apen-nines*, *refer-ring*
- Don't break before a silent final *-ed*: *treat-ed* is fine; *roam-ed* is not

In most modern writing environments, word wrap is automatic. This rule applies primarily to manual typesetting and print contexts.
