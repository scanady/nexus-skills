## Management Consulting & Advisory Firms

Research and reports from these firms carry **trend-confirmation signal**, not breaking news signal. A consulting report on DTC insurance means the trend has crossed the executive agenda threshold — it validates and contextualizes findings from primary sources, but should not be used as the lead signal for any briefing.

**Signal weight guidance**: Route consulting research to 📊 Market & Industry Data or 🌱 Emerging Trends. Never treat a consulting report as breaking news — these typically reflect data that is 6–12 months old by publication date. Use them to add quantification and strategic framing to trends already surfaced by primary sources.

| Firm | Site | Strongest DTC Insurance Content | Signal Type |
|------|------|--------------------------------|-------------|
| **Oliver Wyman** | `oliverwyman.com` | Actuarial modeling, reinsurance, underwriting innovation, protection gap analysis — closest to primary for technical underwriting trends | Trend confirmation, quantified |
| **McKinsey & Company** | `mckinsey.com` | Annual insurance outlook, digital distribution trends, consumer behavior research, carrier technology adoption | Trend confirmation, quantified |
| **Deloitte** | `deloitte.com` | Insurance industry outlook, regulatory compliance, insurtech investment analysis, digital transformation | Trend confirmation, quantified |
| **Bain & Company** | `bain.com` | NPS benchmarks in financial services/insurance, customer loyalty, DTC experience quality | Consumer sentiment benchmarking |
| **Accenture** | `accenture.com` | Technology in insurance, embedded insurance research, AI in underwriting, digital distribution | Tech trend confirmation |
| **BCG** | `bcg.com` | Insurance distribution strategy, life insurance market analysis, emerging market trends | Trend confirmation |
| **EY** | `ey.com` | Insurance regulatory outlook, tax treatment of insurance products, M&A activity | Regulatory and M&A context |
| **KPMG** | `kpmg.com` | Insurance sector outlook, digital transformation, compliance | Trend confirmation |
| **PwC** | `pwc.com` | Insurance industry trends, consumer trust research, financial services regulation | Consumer trust and regulatory |

**When to include**: When a consulting report directly quantifies a trend already surfaced in the briefing (e.g., a McKinsey stat on DTC channel growth confirms what Reddit and trade press are signaling). When a report is the first to put a number on something with no other quantified source.

**When to skip**: When the consulting report is the only source for a trend. When the report's data is clearly based on prior-year figures that predate the current research period.
