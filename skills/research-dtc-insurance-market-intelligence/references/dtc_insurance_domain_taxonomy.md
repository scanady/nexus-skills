# DTC Insurance Domain Taxonomy

Categorization framework for DTC life insurance developments. Use these categories when organizing research findings in Step 4. Select 4–7 categories that fit the actual research — skip any category with no findings.

---

## Category Definitions

### 📦 Product & Underwriting

**What belongs here**: New product launches from DTC carriers. Changes to existing coverage terms, face amount limits, or rider availability. Underwriting guideline updates (lab requirements, knockout criteria, algorithm changes). Accelerated underwriting expansions or contractions. No-exam and instant-issue developments. Pricing changes (rate increases, rate decreases, promotional pricing). New coverage types entering the DTC channel (simplified issue, final expense DTC, group term DTC).

**Impact dimensions to assess**:
- Who can now be covered that couldn't before? (or vice versa)
- How does this affect price competitiveness at the consumer level?
- Does this create or close a coverage gap in the DTC market?

**Example items**: Carrier expands accelerated underwriting to $2M face amounts, DTC platform removes medical exam requirement for applicants up to age 55, carrier introduces new living benefit rider in online channel, pricing update to online term portfolio.

---

### 💻 Distribution & Technology

**What belongs here**: New DTC platform launches or feature updates. API-based distribution partnerships (embedded insurance in apps, payroll, banks). Digital broker and comparison tool updates. AI and ML in underwriting or application experience. Instant-issue technology improvements. Voice, mobile, or multimodal application experiences. Real-time decisioning improvements. CX and conversion rate optimizations in the online path. New integrations between carriers and comparison platforms.

**Impact dimensions to assess**:
- Does this change where or how consumers encounter and purchase life insurance?
- Does this reduce friction in the application experience?
- Does it expand or contract distribution reach for DTC carriers?

**Example items**: Payroll provider adds embedded term life insurance, comparison platform adds real-time quote API for net-of-fee pricing, DTC carrier launches instant-issue decisions up to $500K using alternative data, digital agent platform integrates with 3 new carriers.

---

### 💰 Funding & M&A

**What belongs here**: Venture capital rounds in insurtech companies (especially DTC or distribution-focused). Strategic acquisitions by carriers of DTC platforms or insurtech companies. Mergers between distributors or carriers with DTC implications. Partnerships involving capital allocation (enterprise agreements, distribution exclusives). IPO signals or publicly-traded carrier capital moves affecting DTC strategy. Carrier investments in distribution technology. Private equity activity in the life insurance distribution ecosystem.

**Impact dimensions to assess**:
- Does this signal where capital thinks the DTC market is going?
- Does it change the competitive landscape (fewer players, stronger competitors, new entrants)?
- What does it imply about valuation expectations in the DTC channel?

**Example items**: Insurtech raises $40M Series B for DTC term life platform, traditional carrier acquires digital broker, PE firm rolls up independent digital agencies, public carrier announces DTC-focused strategic initiative with $50M commitment.

---

### ⚖️ Regulation & Compliance

**What belongs here**: NAIC model law development and adoption (especially suitability, replacement, product design rules). State insurance department bulletins and rate filings with DTC implications. State Attorney General consumer protection actions against DTC carriers or platforms. Federal legislation affecting life insurance (ERISA, tax treatment, SECURE Act changes). IRS guidance on tax treatment of life insurance products (COLI, MEC rules, 7702 updates). Market conduct examinations and consent orders. FTC actions on marketing or advertising practices. Consumer disclosure requirement changes. New state requirements for accelerated underwriting or digital applications.

**Impact dimensions to assess**:
- When does this take effect? (pipeline vs. active)
- Who is directly required to act? (carriers, distributors, both)
- What compliance action is required and on what timeline?

**Example items**: NAIC adopts model regulation on AI in underwriting (proposed), NY DFS issues guidance on use of credit data in life insurance underwriting, Congress introduces legislation to expand life insurance tax advantages for middle-income consumers, CA requires new disclosures for online life insurance applications.

---

### 📊 Market & Industry Data

**What belongs here**: LIMRA sales statistics (individual life insurance application activity, face amount trends, channel mix). ACLI industry aggregate data. AM Best market reports and carrier financial strength rating changes. McKinsey, Deloitte, or Oliver Wyman life insurance industry studies. Swiss Re or Munich Re mortality and protection gap research. Competitive market share data. Consumer survey data on life insurance awareness, purchase intent, or ownership rates. Coverage gap and underinsurance statistics. Distribution channel performance comparisons.

**Impact dimensions to assess**:
- Does this data validate or challenge current market assumptions?
- What trends in consumer behavior or market structure does this confirm?
- Are there opportunities or threats implied in the data?

**Example items**: LIMRA Q3 individual life insurance application activity up 4% YoY, ACLI reports record face amount in force, McKinsey reports DTC channel now represents 18% of new term life policies, Swiss Re estimates US protection gap at $17T, AM Best downgrades mid-tier carrier.

---

### 💬 Consumer & Sentiment

**What belongs here**: Reddit thread analysis from r/LifeInsurance, r/personalfinance, r/financialindependence. Consumer review patterns on Trustpilot, Google, or app stores for DTC carriers. Social media sentiment about specific carriers or products. Consumer complaint filing trends from state departments. Consumer confusion or misunderstanding patterns that signal education or CX gaps. Viral social content (positive or negative) featuring life insurance. Generational sentiment shifts toward life insurance ownership. Trust signals and distrust patterns in the DTC channel.

**Key distinction**: Individual Reddit policy questions are NOT data points. A recurring theme across 10+ threads IS signal — note the pattern and quote representative comments.

**Impact dimensions to assess**:
- What friction points are consumers actually experiencing in the DTC process?
- Which carriers or products are winning consumer sentiment and why?
- Are there misconceptions that affect purchase rates at scale?

**Example items**: Multiple Reddit threads identify underwriting rescission after claim as top complaint, r/personalfinance "best term life recommendation" thread reveals strong Policygenius brand recognition among respondents, TikTok video warning about graded death benefit products goes viral, consumer trust in online life insurance application has increased per survey.

---

### 🌱 Emerging Trends

**What belongs here**: Distribution channel innovations that are gaining early traction. Demographic shifts affecting DTC market (Gen Z entering peak purchase age, millennials hitting 35–45). Behavioral pattern changes in life insurance discovery or purchase. Technology approaches beginning to show adoption (embedded, conversational AI in underwriting, real-time income verification). Cross-industry signals that will affect life insurance (open banking data for underwriting, wearables data, employer benefit evolution). Early signals of consumer segment shifts (gig economy workers, self-employed, underserved demographics). Business model innovations too early to categorize as mainstream.

**Impact dimensions to assess**:
- Is this a durable trend or a moment? What evidence supports durability?
- What's the likely timeframe for mainstream impact?
- Which current DTC players are best/worst positioned if this trend matures?

**Example items**: Two insurtech firms now offering income-replacement structured DTC life policies (early), embedded insurance in payroll platforms showing strong conversion rates per operator data, Gen Z consumers showing stronger awareness of term life than Millennial cohort at same age, BNPL-style premium financing emerging in DTC applications.

---

### 🔭 Horizon — Regulatory Pipeline & Early Signals

**What belongs here**: Items tagged `[HORIZON]` — proposed regulations not yet final, legislation in committee, early-stage trends building below the surface, pilot programs from major carriers, funding rounds for pre-revenue startups signaling market direction. Products in development that have been announced but not launched. Actuarial or academic research that will affect underwriting in the next 1–3 years. NAIC task force workstreams that haven't produced guidance yet. State legislative sessions beginning to consider life insurance–related bills.

Critical: **These are tomorrow's stories.** Include them here before they've widely impacted the market. They deserve dedicated attention because they provide maximum lead time for response.

**Example items**: NAIC AI & Innovation Working Group examining algorithmic underwriting guidance (no model law yet), California legislature introduced bill requiring approval for ML-based underwriting decisions, three states considering legislation to extend free-look period for online purchases to 30 days, three pre-revenue insurtechs raised seed rounds for behavior-based life insurance pricing.

## Cross-Category Guidance

### When Items Span Multiple Categories

Some developments have implications across categories — briefly note the primary category but call out secondary implications:
- A new AI underwriting platform (💻 Distribution & Technology) may also have regulatory implications (⚖️) and consumer sentiment signals (💬)
- A LIMRA data report (📊 Market Data) may confirm an emerging trend (🌱) and have strategic takeaway implications
- A carrier funding round (💰 Funding) may signal a product direction (📦 Product) and distribution strategy change (💻)

### Priority Order for Contested Items

When uncertain about placement, default to the category that captures the **primary action** (what happened) rather than the implication:
1. If a carrier launched a product → 📦 Product & Underwriting (even if noteworthy as distribution innovation)
2. If a regulator took action → ⚖️ Regulation & Compliance (even if it has distribution implications)
3. If it's funding news → 💰 Funding & M&A (even if the startup has a DTC product)
