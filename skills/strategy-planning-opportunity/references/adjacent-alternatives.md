# Adjacent Alternatives

Use this reference when the core idea is not the best expression of the underlying insight. Suggest alternatives that preserve the valuable part of the opportunity while improving market, distribution, economics, or fit.

## Alternative Types

| Type | Use When | Example Shift |
|------|----------|---------------|
| Customer pivot | Pain is real but current buyer is weak | From consumers to professionals with budget |
| Pain-point pivot | Audience is attractive but problem is low urgency | From information tool to workflow automation |
| Distribution pivot | Product is plausible but channel is weak | From paid ads to partner-led or community-led motion |
| Business model pivot | Value exists but monetization is poor | From free content to paid templates, data, or services |
| Scope reduction | Idea is too broad or complex | From platform to single high-value wedge |
| Productized service | Software risk is high but buyer pain is immediate | From SaaS to done-for-you service that informs product |
| Data/content wedge | Trust and demand need to be built first | From product launch to benchmark report or newsletter |
| Internal tool first | External market uncertain but user's own need is clear | Build for self, then package if repeated demand appears |
| Premium niche | Market is small but willingness to pay is high | From mass-market app to specialized B2B workflow |
| Kill-and-reallocate | Insight is weak and better options exist | Remove idea and shift resources to stronger portfolio asset |

## How to Generate Better Alternatives

For each alternative, change exactly one or two variables:

1. **Customer** — Who has a more painful version of this problem?
2. **Job** — What higher-value job sits next to this idea?
3. **Channel** — Where does the user already have reach or credibility?
4. **Offer** — What smaller paid thing could validate demand faster?
5. **Model** — Could this be subscription, service, marketplace, data, licensing, lead-gen, or embedded workflow?
6. **Wedge** — What narrow entry point could expand into the broader vision?
7. **Timing** — Should this be parked until a regulation, platform, customer behavior, or tool changes?
8. **Portfolio synergy** — Does this become stronger if paired with an existing asset?

## Alternative Quality Bar

A good alternative must:

- Improve at least one weak scorecard dimension.
- Preserve the strongest insight behind the original idea.
- Fit the user's constraints better than the original.
- Have a concrete next validation step.
- Be meaningfully different, not just a reworded version of the same idea.

## Output Pattern

Use this table in the final assessment:

| Alternative | What Changes | Why It May Be Better | New Risk |
|-------------|--------------|----------------------|----------|
| [Name] | [customer/model/channel/scope] | [improved score dimension] | [new failure mode] |

Then name the best alternative if one is clearly superior:

**Best adjacent bet:** [Alternative] — [one-sentence rationale]

## Companion Workflows

If the user needs more than alternatives, suggest but do not require a companion skill:

- Use an opportunity researcher/creator when they want a broader list of new ideas.
- Use a portfolio prioritizer when comparing many assessed opportunities.
- Use a validation experiment designer when the next action is to test demand.
- Use a market research specialist when the assessment depends on current evidence.
