# Financial Sketch

Use this reference to create quick, decision-grade economics for an opportunity. The goal is not a full model; it is to reveal whether the business can plausibly support the user's goal.

## Core Outputs

Produce these items when relevant:

1. **Revenue model** — subscription, transaction fee, services, usage-based, ads, affiliate, licensing, hardware, data, marketplace take rate, or hybrid.
2. **Buyer and price** — who pays, why they pay, and a plausible price range.
3. **Volume driver** — customers, seats, transactions, projects, leads, impressions, or usage units.
4. **Gross margin logic** — software, labor, fulfillment, payment fees, support, data, infrastructure, or partner costs.
5. **Acquisition logic** — channel, expected conversion bottleneck, sales cycle, CAC risk, and payback intuition.
6. **Break-even path** — number of customers or deals needed to cover expected operating costs.
7. **Base/upside/downside scenarios** — rough ranges with assumptions and decision implications.

## Quick Equations

Use simple equations and state assumptions:

- **Annual revenue:** customers × annual price × retention adjustment
- **Marketplace revenue:** transactions × average order value × take rate
- **Services revenue:** projects × average contract value × delivery capacity
- **Content/community revenue:** audience × conversion rate × ARPU
- **Gross profit:** revenue × gross margin
- **Contribution profit:** revenue − variable delivery costs − direct acquisition costs
- **Break-even customers:** fixed monthly costs ÷ monthly gross profit per customer
- **CAC payback:** CAC ÷ monthly gross profit per customer

## Scenario Pattern

Use this compact format:

| Scenario | What Must Be True | Revenue / Margin Sketch | Implication |
|----------|-------------------|-------------------------|-------------|
| Downside | Weak conversion, low price, high churn, or hard delivery | [rough range] | [kill/pivot/run small] |
| Base | Reasonable niche adoption with known channels | [rough range] | [validate/launch/run] |
| Upside | Strong pull, scalable distribution, expansion, or premium pricing | [rough range] | [grow/raise/scale] |

## Venture vs. Cash-Flow Interpretation

### Venture-scale opportunities
Look for:
- Path to very large annual revenue or enterprise value
- Strong growth rate and large reachable market
- High gross margins or strong operating leverage
- Defensibility or network/data/platform compounding
- Distribution that can scale beyond founder-led sales

Red flags:
- Services-heavy delivery without productization path
- Small capped market
- Slow enterprise sales with low ACV
- No retention or expansion logic

### Bootstrapped / cash-flow opportunities
Look for:
- Fast path to revenue
- High trust customer access
- Manageable support and delivery burden
- Premium niche willingness to pay
- Low fixed costs and quick break-even

Red flags:
- Low-priced product with expensive support
- High churn
- Founder time trapped in custom delivery
- Paid acquisition dependence before economics are proven

### Micro / side-project opportunities
Look for:
- Tiny but reachable audience
- Very low operating burden
- Reusable assets or portfolio synergy
- Clear learning, audience, or option value
- Fast kill criteria

Red flags:
- Ongoing maintenance without cash, learning, or strategic value
- Complex compliance or customer support
- Requires constant content or sales effort the user will not sustain

## Assumption Quality

Label assumptions:

- **Known:** Supported by user data, customer evidence, actual pricing, or real traction.
- **Comparable:** Borrowed from analogous businesses or public benchmarks.
- **Estimated:** Plausible but unverified; needs validation.
- **Speculative:** Weakly grounded; should lower confidence.

Never let speculative assumptions drive a high-confidence grow recommendation.
