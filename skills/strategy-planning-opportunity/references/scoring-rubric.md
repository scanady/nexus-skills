# Scoring Rubric

Use this rubric to produce a scale-aware opportunity score. Score each dimension from 0–10, then apply weights based on the opportunity type and user goal. A score is a decision aid, not a substitute for judgment.

## Default Weights

| Dimension | Venture-Scale | Bootstrapped / Cash Flow | Micro / Side Project | What Good Looks Like |
|-----------|--------------:|-------------------------:|---------------------:|----------------------|
| Customer pain and urgency | 15% | 15% | 15% | Frequent, painful, budgeted, actively searched-for problem |
| Market structure and upside | 15% | 10% | 5% | Enough reachable demand for the user's goal; favorable fragmentation or growth |
| Distribution advantage | 15% | 15% | 20% | Clear path to customers through owned audience, network, channel, SEO, partnerships, or sales motion |
| Monetization and unit economics | 15% | 20% | 20% | Buyer can pay; margins and payback work; pricing is plausible |
| Defensibility and compounding | 10% | 5% | 5% | Data, workflow lock-in, brand, network effects, switching costs, scale, or compounding distribution |
| Timing and tailwinds | 10% | 10% | 10% | Enabling technology, regulation, behavior shift, platform change, or budget movement supports now |
| Founder/opportunity fit | 10% | 15% | 15% | User has domain insight, network, credibility, skill, motivation, or unfair access |
| Execution complexity | 5% | 5% | 5% | Build, operations, legal, sales, and support complexity fit available resources |
| Portfolio fit and opportunity cost | 5% | 5% | 5% | Complements current portfolio and beats alternatives for time/capital allocation |

## Score Bands

| Score | Tier | Default Interpretation |
|------:|------|------------------------|
| 85–100 | Exceptional | Strong candidate to grow or launch aggressively; still validate critical assumptions |
| 70–84 | Strong | Worth pursuing if constraints fit; define a focused next milestone |
| 55–69 | Conditional | Promising but needs validation, narrowing, or pivot before serious investment |
| 40–54 | Weak | Park, pivot, or run only if it has special non-financial value |
| 0–39 | Poor | Kill or remove unless new evidence changes the thesis |

## Dimension Scoring Anchors

### Customer pain and urgency
- **9–10:** Severe, frequent, expensive pain; buyers already spend money or hack together workarounds.
- **6–8:** Real pain, but urgency, frequency, or budget is uneven.
- **3–5:** Mild pain, nice-to-have, or unclear buyer.
- **0–2:** No specific customer or painful job identified.

### Market structure and upside
- **9–10:** Large or rapidly growing market with clear wedge and plausible expansion path.
- **6–8:** Solid niche with enough upside for the user's goal.
- **3–5:** Small, crowded, or structurally hard market; may still fit micro goals.
- **0–2:** Too tiny, shrinking, inaccessible, or dominated by impossible-to-displace incumbents.

### Distribution advantage
- **9–10:** User has unfair access to customers or a repeatable low-cost acquisition channel.
- **6–8:** Plausible acquisition motion with testable channels.
- **3–5:** Generic distribution plan or expensive channels with uncertain conversion.
- **0–2:** No credible path to reach customers.

### Monetization and unit economics
- **9–10:** Clear buyer, price, gross margin, retention, and payback logic.
- **6–8:** Pricing plausible; assumptions need validation.
- **3–5:** Monetization exists but is weak, indirect, or operationally heavy.
- **0–2:** No buyer, weak willingness to pay, or economics likely fail.

### Defensibility and compounding
- **9–10:** Strong compounding advantage: data, network, switching costs, brand, workflow lock-in, or platform scale.
- **6–8:** Some durability if execution succeeds.
- **3–5:** Useful but easy to copy; can still be acceptable for cash-flow goals.
- **0–2:** Commodity with no durable advantage.

### Timing and tailwinds
- **9–10:** Clear now-or-never shift creates demand or enables a new solution.
- **6–8:** Favorable trend supports adoption.
- **3–5:** Timing is neutral or speculative.
- **0–2:** Timing is wrong; customer behavior, regulation, or technology is not ready.

### Founder/opportunity fit
- **9–10:** User has rare insight, credibility, network, or execution edge.
- **6–8:** Good skill and interest fit.
- **3–5:** Fit is generic; user can learn but has no edge.
- **0–2:** Poor fit with user's constraints, motivation, or capabilities.

### Execution complexity
- **9–10:** Simple path to test and operate with available resources.
- **6–8:** Manageable complexity with known risks.
- **3–5:** Heavy build, operations, compliance, support, or sales burden.
- **0–2:** Complexity exceeds likely resources.

### Portfolio fit and opportunity cost
- **9–10:** Strong synergy with existing assets and beats other portfolio options.
- **6–8:** Good standalone fit; limited conflicts.
- **3–5:** Some fit but competes with better opportunities.
- **0–2:** Distracting, duplicative, or misaligned.

## Confidence Rating

Use **High** when the assessment has strong customer evidence, comparable benchmarks, clear economics, and direct user constraints.
Use **Medium** when the idea is specific but relies on several estimates.
Use **Low** when the user provides a thin description, market facts are missing, or the core customer/problem is vague.

Always explain what evidence would increase confidence.
