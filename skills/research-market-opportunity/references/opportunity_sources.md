# Opportunity Sources

Use a source mix similar to a weekly AI news workflow, but tune the scan for opportunity discovery instead of headline summarization. Prefer primary sources, fresh signals, and communities where real pain appears before polished market reports.

## Source Mix Rule

For fresh opportunity research, collect signals from at least four source types:

1. Technology and AI news
2. Startup and funding news
3. Developer/open-source ecosystems
4. Customer/community pain sources
5. Embedded/IoT ecosystems when relevant
6. Regulatory or industry change sources when relevant

## Tier 1: Technology and Startup Signal Sources

| Source | URL | Best For | Opportunity Signal |
|--------|-----|----------|--------------------|

| VentureBeat AI | https://venturebeat.com/category/ai/ | Enterprise AI, products, platform changes | Enterprise adoption patterns and workflow gaps |
| The Verge AI | https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence | Consumer AI, platforms, product shifts | Consumer behavior shifts and product friction |
| MIT Technology Review AI | https://www.technologyreview.com/topic/artificial-intelligence/ | Deeper analysis, research implications | Longer-range shifts and societal constraints |
| Hacker News | https://news.ycombinator.com/ | Developer reactions and emerging tools | Complaints, enthusiasm, technical pain, founder narratives |
| Product Hunt | https://www.producthunt.com/ | New software launches | Crowded categories, launch copy, underserved users in comments |
| Indie Hackers | https://www.indiehackers.com/ | Bootstrapped business ideas | Micro-SaaS economics, founder pain, niche opportunities |

## Tier 4: Customer Pain and Market Friction Sources

| Source Type | Where to Look | Signal to Extract |
|-------------|---------------|-------------------|
| Forums | Reddit, Stack Overflow, vendor forums, Home Assistant Community, Espressif forums | Repeated complaints, workarounds, unanswered questions |
| Reviews | G2, Capterra, app stores, Chrome Web Store, Shopify App Store, AWS Marketplace | Feature gaps, support pain, pricing objections |
| Job postings | LinkedIn, Indeed, Y Combinator Work at a Startup, Wellfound | Emerging roles, manual workflows, tool budgets |
| RFPs and procurement | Government portals, school districts, municipal contracts | Budgeted needs and compliance requirements |
| Regulations | Federal Register, EU updates, state rules, industry bodies | New compliance work and reporting burdens |
| Communities | Discords, Slack groups, subreddits, newsletters | Language customers use before a category exists |

## Source Handling Standards

- Prefer original sources over aggregators when making factual claims.
- Record publication date or observation date for every material signal.
- Use community comments as pain evidence, not as market-size proof.
- Treat funding as a category signal, not proof that an adjacent idea is good.
- Distinguish launch volume from customer demand.
- Do not use a source unless it contributes a signal to a specific opportunity.
