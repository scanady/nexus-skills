# Financial Services and Life Insurance Review Checks

Use this checklist for common risk points in life insurance and related financial marketing.

## Product and Benefit Claims

| Checkpoint | Pass criteria | Red flag examples |
|---|---|---|
| Benefit statement precision | Benefits described with clear conditions and limitations | "Guaranteed wealth", "no-risk growth" |
| Guarantee language | Guarantees tied to specific contract terms and issuing entity obligations | Guarantee implied without conditions |
| Policy value projections | Projection framing includes assumptions and variability caveats | Implied certainty of projected outcomes |
| Tax language | Tax statements framed as general information with advice caveat | Definitive tax outcomes for all customers |

## Replacement and Comparison Sensitivities

| Checkpoint | Pass criteria | Red flag examples |
|---|---|---|
| Product comparisons | Basis of comparison is clear, current, and apples-to-apples | Competitor comparison without methodology |
| Replacement implications | Surrender charges, contestability, and loss of benefits are clearly noted when relevant | Encouraging replacement without tradeoff disclosure |
| Cost representation | Fees, charges, and premium obligations are represented accurately | "Low cost" with omitted fee context |

## Sales Practice and Suitability Messaging

| Checkpoint | Pass criteria | Red flag examples |
|---|---|---|
| Target audience clarity | Intended customer profile is clear and consistent with product use case | One-size-fits-all suitability statements |
| Advice framing | Distinguishes educational content from individualized advice | Implicit personalized recommendation without context |
| Producer claims | Agent/advisor credentials and role claims are accurate and verifiable | Inflated expertise or unverified credential references |

## Required Documentation Signals

- Approved product fact source for each claim
- Legal-approved disclosure blocks by channel
- Versioned approval history for final creative
- Final distribution channel map and audience definition

## Escalation Triggers

Escalate to legal/compliance leadership when any of the following appear:

- Novel guarantee framing or nonstandard performance claim
- Cross-jurisdiction campaign with conflicting local requirements
- Comparative claims lacking robust substantiation package
- Scripted oral claims that cannot be effectively disclosed in-channel
