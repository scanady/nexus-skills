# Regulatory Control Matrix

Use this matrix to map findings to likely regulatory or supervisory themes before assigning risk.

## Scope Note

This matrix is an operational review aid for marketing and compliance teams. It is not legal advice and does not replace counsel review.

## Control Families

| Control family | Core question | Typical evidence | High-risk trigger examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| Truthful and not misleading | Would a reasonable consumer be misled by wording, framing, or omission? | Product facts, approved copy, source data, disclosure text | Absolute claims, implied guarantees, selective performance presentation |
| Fair balance | Are material limitations, exclusions, and risks given appropriate prominence? | Layout comps, script timing, disclosure placement notes | Benefits in headline, risks buried in fine print |
| Claim substantiation | Can every factual claim be supported now with documented evidence? | Studies, actuarial sources, internal analytics, filing support | Unsupported superlatives, unverifiable statistics |
| Suitability and audience fit | Is the message appropriate for the intended audience and channel? | Audience definition, segmentation rules, sales practice guidance | Universal suitability language, over-broad audience claims |
| Disclosure adequacy | Are mandatory disclosures complete, clear, and proximate to triggering claims? | Disclosure library, legal templates, channel standards | Missing required legends, disclosures detached from claims |
| Supervision and approval trail | Is the material routed and retained per policy? | Approval log, version history, reviewer sign-off | Launch without approval record, missing retention metadata |
| Privacy and consent | Are lead capture and remarketing statements aligned with consent and privacy commitments? | Privacy notice, consent copy, form flows | Hidden consent language, unclear data-use statements |

## Control-to-Rating Guidance

| Observation profile | Suggested rating |
|---|---|
| Minor wording issues; evidence complete; disclosures adequate | Green |
| Multiple moderate issues; remediations needed before launch | Yellow |
| Material misleading risk, missing substantiation, or missing mandatory disclosures | Red |

## Reviewer Prompt Snippets

- Which specific claim in this asset is most likely to create a misleading net impression?
- What supporting evidence exists for each quantitative or comparative statement?
- Where is the closest disclosure to the triggering claim, and is that proximity defensible?
- What would a regulator likely identify as the first high-priority concern?
