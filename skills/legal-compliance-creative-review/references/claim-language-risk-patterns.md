# Claim Language Risk Patterns

Use this guide to detect risky phrasing and replace it with compliant alternatives.

## High-Risk Patterns and Safer Moves

| Risk pattern | Why risky | Safer direction |
|---|---|---|
| Absolute certainty (always, guaranteed results, risk-free) | Can create misleading net impression and overpromise outcomes | Use conditional, bounded language tied to product terms |
| Undefined superlatives (best, highest, unbeatable) | Requires strong substantiation and comparison basis | Replace with specific, supportable descriptors |
| Selective upside framing | Omits material limitations and downside factors | Pair benefits with proximate limitations and assumptions |
| Ambiguous time outcomes (fast growth, immediate security) | Implies timeline certainty without basis | Specify conditions, ranges, and timing uncertainties |
| Implied regulatory endorsement | Suggests external approval of marketing claims | Remove endorsement implication and clarify source |

## Rewrite Patterns

| Instead of | Prefer |
|---|---|
| "Guaranteed high returns" | "Designed for long-term growth potential, with outcomes that vary by market conditions and policy terms." |
| "Best choice for retirement" | "Can be appropriate for certain retirement planning goals, depending on needs, time horizon, and risk tolerance." |
| "No downside" | "Includes protections described in the contract, alongside conditions, limits, and tradeoffs." |
| "Everyone should switch now" | "May be worth evaluating for eligible customers after comparing costs, benefits, and replacement implications." |

## Evidence Check per Claim

For each claim, verify:

1. Factual source exists and is current.
2. Language matches source scope exactly.
3. Material limitations are included.
4. Disclosure appears near the claim.
5. Tone and emphasis are balanced.
