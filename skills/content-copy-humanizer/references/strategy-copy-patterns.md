# Strategy, Brand, and Coaching Copy Patterns

Load this when text is grammatically clean but still feels unnatural, especially in
brand, leadership, coaching, strategy, executive, or marketing copy. These are the
patterns that survive a basic AI-tell scrub and still ring false.

Core test for every sentence here: would a real person in this context actually say
this, or is the sentence using polished abstraction to avoid naming the action,
decision, tradeoff, or consequence?

## 27. Abstract imperative compression
Tell: a plain verb plus an abstract noun, producing a command that sounds meaningful
but does not say what to do.

Watch: name the bet, name the pressure, name the tension, name the real question,
hold the tension, sit with the complexity, surface the truth, make the implicit
explicit, create space for, get clear on, orient around.

Guardrail: do not rewrite ordinary concrete uses. "Name the file," "name the owner,"
"hold the meeting," and "define the acceptance criteria" are natural, because the
noun is specific and observable.

Before:
> Name the bet before you move.

After:
> Say what decision you are making, what must be true for it to work, and what you do if it does not.

Before:
> Name the real pressure.

After:
> Identify what is actually creating pressure: timing, money, role conflict, customer risk, or trust.

## 28. Faux-profound abstraction
Tell: the sentence gestures at depth while withholding the concrete thing the reader
needs: what changes, who does it, why it matters, how anyone will know.

Watch: unlock, activate, elevate, catalyze, journey, transformation, intentionality,
alignment, resonance, container, the work, the moment, the path forward, meaningful
change, move with intention, grounded, spacious.

Before:
> We create space for leaders to unlock clarity and move with intention.

After:
> We help leaders separate the decision from the noise around it, test the tradeoffs, and choose a next step.

## 29. Vague depth intensifiers
Tell: "real," "true," and "deep" pretend to add insight while leaving the actual
pressure, question, or choice unnamed.

Watch: real pressure, real question, real choice, true north, deeper work, deeper
truth, the thing under the thing, what is really going on, what matters most, at the
deepest level.

Before:
> We listen for the real pressure, the real question, and the real choice.

After:
> We ask what changed, which decision is stuck, and which tradeoff the leader is avoiding.

## 30. Mechanism-free transformation claims
Tell: the copy promises a good result without naming how it happens.

Watch: builds trust, creates alignment, strengthens leadership capacity, drives
meaningful outcomes, enables transformation, fosters growth, supports resilience,
creates lasting impact.

Before:
> The engagement creates alignment and strengthens leadership capacity.

After:
> By the end, the team has agreed on the decision owner, the tradeoffs, and the next three conversations.

## Rewrite heuristic

If a phrase feels profound, ask what it means in observable terms:

- Who acts?
- What decision, behavior, or conversation changes?
- What pressure, constraint, or tradeoff is actually present?
- How would a reader know the claim happened?

If the answer is missing, rewrite until the answer is visible. If you cannot find
the answer in the source, the copy was empty; flag it rather than inventing one.
