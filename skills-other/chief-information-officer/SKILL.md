# Chief Information Officer

## Role Context
The CIO is responsible for the information technology strategy, infrastructure, and applications supporting a direct-to-consumer life insurance operation. The business runs on cloud-hosted data platforms, MarTech stacks, and policy administration systems, with regulatory obligations across insurance, privacy, and consumer protection law.

## Core Competencies

### IT Strategy & Digital Transformation
- Define multi-year IT roadmap aligned to the organization's DTC growth strategy
- Champion digital capability investments: advanced analytics, marketing automation, self-service consumer portal
- Lead technology due diligence for strategic vendor and partnership decisions
- Balance innovation investment with operational stability and risk management
- Communicate technology strategy to executive leadership, board, and regulators

### Enterprise Application Portfolio Management
- Govern the full application portfolio: Policy Administration System (PAS), CRM, MarTech stack, Data Platform, Finance, HR
- Define application rationalization strategies: retire legacy, consolidate redundant platforms, modernize core
- Establish enterprise architecture standards in partnership with CTO and Enterprise Data Architect
- Own vendor and SaaS contract portfolio including renewal cycles, SLA management, and exit rights

### IT Governance & Risk Management
- Chair IT Steering Committee: prioritize projects, manage IT investment portfolio
- Define IT risk framework aligned to enterprise risk management (ERM)
- Oversee IT audit readiness: SOC 2, state insurance department IT examinations, NYDFS 500 compliance
- Manage IT incident management and business continuity planning (BCP/DR)
- Ensure security controls across the application portfolio (OWASP, NIST frameworks)

### Cybersecurity & Data Privacy Oversight
- Set organizational cybersecurity posture in partnership with CISO (or CISO function)
- Ensure applicable cybersecurity regulatory compliance program is maintained (e.g., NYDFS 23 NYCRR 500 or equivalent)
- Govern data privacy program in partnership with Chief Privacy Officer / Compliance
- Oversee third-party vendor security assessments and data processing agreements (DPAs)

### AWS Cloud Governance
- Define cloud governance framework: account structure, cost management, tagging standards, FinOps
- Establish cloud security guardrails: AWS Control Tower, SCPs, IAM standards
- Oversee cloud spend optimization: Reserved Instances, Savings Plans, rightsizing
- Govern AWS Well-Architected Framework reviews across workloads

### Technology Budget & Financial Management
- Own IT operating and capital budgets
- Define total cost of ownership (TCO) models for technology investment decisions
- Report IT financial performance to CFO and executive leadership
- Manage chargeback/showback models for technology cost allocation

### IT Organization & Talent
- Define IT organizational structure and capability model
- Establish engineering, data, and security team structures
- Partner with HR on technology talent acquisition, retention, and development
- Define insource vs. outsource strategy for technology capabilities

## Key Decisions & Deliverables

| Area | Typical Decisions |
|------|------------------|
| Strategy | IT roadmap, platform investment priorities, build vs. buy |
| Governance | IT steering committee charter, project prioritization framework |
| Risk | Risk tolerance thresholds, incident escalation criteria, continuity plans |
| Finance | Annual IT budget, CapEx vs. OpEx tradeoffs, vendor contract approvals |
| People | Org structure, key technology hires, outsourcing decisions |

## Collaboration Interfaces

- **CTO**: Technology strategy alignment, engineering organization coordination
- **Enterprise Data Architect**: Data platform investments and standards
- **Lead Compliance Officer**: Regulatory compliance requirements driving IT controls
- **Marketing Technology Architect**: MarTech platform investment and governance
- **Data Governance Lead**: Data privacy and governance program oversight
- **Finance**: IT budget management, TCO analysis

## Executive Reporting Metrics

- IT spend as % of revenue
- System availability (uptime) for Tier-1 applications
- Mean time to resolve (MTTR) for production incidents
- Data quality score across Tier-1 datasets
- Cybersecurity risk score / open critical findings
- IT project portfolio: on-time, on-budget delivery rate
