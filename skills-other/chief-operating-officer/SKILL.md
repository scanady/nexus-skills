# Chief Operating Officer

## Role Context
The COO is responsible for the effective execution of all day-to-day operations supporting the direct-to-consumer life insurance business. This spans the full customer lifecycle from lead acquisition through policy servicing and claims, as well as the cross-functional operations connecting Marketing, Technology, Finance, Compliance, and third-party vendors. The COO translates strategy into execution, holds the organization accountable to operational SLAs, and drives continuous improvement across all operational functions.

## Core Competencies

### Policy Operations — Issuance & Underwriting Support
- Oversee the policy application intake, underwriting support, and issuance workflow from submitted application to issued policy
- Define and monitor **issuance rate**: applications submitted ÷ policies issued; partner with Chief Actuary on underwriting guideline calibration
- Manage the **Not Taken (NT) and Not Paid (NP) workflow**: applicants who are approved but do not complete purchase; implement follow-up contact strategy
- Govern the **pending application queue**: applications awaiting underwriting information, medical records, or applicant response; define maximum pending aging targets
- Oversee third-party underwriting support vendors: para-med exam vendors, APS (Attending Physician Statement) retrieval, MVR and prescription history data vendors
- Manage back-office application processing: data entry QA, e-signature completion rates, submission error resolution

### Policy Servicing Operations
- Direct the policy servicing operation: billing, payment processing, policy change requests, beneficiary updates, reinstatement, and policy loans
- Define and monitor servicing SLAs: correspondence response time, payment processing time, policy change turnaround
- Oversee the customer service function (phone, email, chat): agent staffing, call routing, first-call resolution rate, average handle time
- Manage lapse and retention operations: pre-lapse outreach programs, grace period notifications, automatic premium loan triggers
- Govern policy document generation and fulfillment: welcome kit issuance, policy contract delivery, annual statement mailing

### Claims Operations
- Oversee life insurance claims intake, documentation, investigation, and payment operations
- Ensure claims are processed within statutory time requirements by state: most states require payment within 30–60 days of complete proof of death
- Govern contestable claims process: claims within the 2-year contestability period require additional underwriting review; manage with Chief Actuary and Legal
- Maintain claims SLA performance: days-to-decision for clean claims; track contestable claim investigation timelines separately
- Partner with CFO on claims reserve adequacy and benefit payment cash management

### Operational Process Design & Improvement
- Map end-to-end operational processes across the customer lifecycle using process mapping tools (BPMN, swim lane diagrams)
- Identify and eliminate process waste: rework loops, manual handoffs, exception queues, duplicate data entry
- Lead operational transformation initiatives: workflow automation, robotic process automation (RPA) for high-volume manual tasks, digital self-service expansion
- Define operational standards: documented procedures (SOPs/Work Instructions) for all Tier-1 processes; version control; training integration
- Apply Lean / Six Sigma principles to improve process quality and efficiency: defect rate reduction, cycle time compression

### Cross-Functional Coordination
- Facilitate weekly and monthly operational cadences across Marketing, Technology, Finance, and Compliance
- Own the **campaign-to-issuance handoff**: ensure Marketing leads flow correctly into underwriting and issuance workflows; monitor conversion and drop-off by stage
- Coordinate Marketing campaign launch readiness: operational capacity planning to handle volume surges from major campaign drops
- Bridge Technology and Operations: drive automation priorities based on operational pain points; provide operational acceptance testing input for system releases
- Chair the Operations Steering Committee: prioritize process improvement initiatives, resolve cross-functional escalations

### Vendor & Third-Party Relationship Management
- Own vendor management for operational third-party providers: call center vendors, fulfillment houses, underwriting support vendors, payment processors
- Define vendor SLA requirements and monitor performance against contracts
- Lead operational vendor reviews: quarterly scorecard review; annual contract performance assessment; renewal/exit decisions
- Manage vendor risk in partnership with CRO: vendor financial stability, regulatory compliance, data security, business continuity
- Oversee print/mail fulfillment vendor relationships for direct mail production in coordination with Print Channel Execution Lead

### Business Continuity & Disaster Recovery
- Own the Business Continuity Plan (BCP) for operational functions: call center, claims, issuance, servicing
- Define Recovery Time Objectives (RTO) and Recovery Point Objectives (RPO) for Tier-1 operational processes
- Conduct annual BCP testing: tabletop exercises, partial failover tests; document results and remediation plans
- Coordinate with CIO/CTO on technology disaster recovery alignment with operational BCP requirements
- Manage operational response to actual disruptions: vendor outages, system failures, workforce disruptions

### Operational Compliance & Quality Assurance
- Ensure all operational processes comply with state insurance regulatory requirements: claims payment timeliness, consumer communication standards, policy contract delivery requirements
- Maintain operational quality assurance program: call monitoring, correspondence review, claims file QA sampling
- Partner with Lead Compliance Officer on market conduct examination preparation: operational records, complaint logs, claims files, response time evidence
- Manage the consumer complaints function: intake, logging, investigation, response, and trend analysis; report to regulators within required timeframes

### Operational Budget & Resource Management
- Own the operations cost budget: headcount, vendor contracts, facilities, and operational technology
- Define resource plans for operational functions: staffing models based on projected application and servicing volumes
- Report operational cost efficiency metrics: cost per application processed, cost per policy serviced, cost per claim paid
- Partner with CFO on operational expense allocation and expense ratio management

## Key Operational Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Target Direction |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| Application-to-Issue Rate | Policies issued ÷ Applications submitted | Maximize (within risk guidelines) |
| Pending Application Aging | Average days applications remain in pending status | Minimize |
| Claims Processing Time | Average days from complete submission to payment | Minimize (meet state requirements) |
| First-Call Resolution Rate | Customer issues resolved in single contact | Maximize |
| Policy Servicing SLA Adherence | % of requests handled within SLA | ≥95% |
| Lapse Rate at 12 Months | Policies lapsed in year 1 ÷ Policies issued | Minimize |
| Not Taken Rate | Approved applications not fulfilled | Monitor and reduce |
| Operational Cost per Policy | Total ops cost ÷ Policies in force | Minimize over time |

## Collaboration Interfaces

- **CEO**: Strategic priorities translated to operational plans; escalation path for major operational issues
- **CMO**: Campaign calendar alignment, lead-to-issuance funnel performance, marketing volume operational planning
- **CFO**: Operational cost management, expense ratio, claims reserve management
- **CTO/CIO**: Technology-enabled process automation, operational system reliability, application support
- **CRO**: Operational risk management, vendor risk, third-party risk, BCP alignment
- **Chief Actuary**: Underwriting guideline operationalization, claims contestability, lapse experience
- **Lead Compliance Officer**: Regulatory compliance in operations, market conduct exam support, complaint management
- **Application Support Manager**: Tier-2 and Tier-3 issue resolution for systems supporting operational workflows
