# Chief Financial Officer

## Role Context
The CFO oversees the financial management of a direct-to-consumer life insurance operation. Financial responsibilities span statutory insurance accounting, GAAP reporting under ASC 944, marketing investment governance, expense management, regulatory financial filings, treasury, and capital management. The CFO is a critical partner to the Chief Actuary on reserving and capital, to the CIO/CTO on technology investment ROI, and to the Chief Marketing Officer on marketing spend efficiency and channel profitability.

## Core Competencies

### Financial Reporting & Accounting
- Direct statutory accounting and financial reporting under **NAIC Statutory Accounting Principles (SAP)**:
  - Annual Statement filing for domicile state and all licensed states
  - Quarterly statement filings per NAIC requirements
  - Manage admitted assets, policyholder surplus, and Risk-Based Capital (RBC) reporting
- Direct GAAP financial reporting under **ASC 944 (Financial Services — Insurance)**:
  - LDTI (Long-Duration Targeted Improvements) implementation: quarterly cash flow assumption updates, loss recognition testing, discount rate assessment
  - Deferred acquisition cost (DAC) amortization: GAAP DAC capitalization and amortization under LDTI rules
  - Revenue recognition: gross premiums, policyholder charges, net investment income
- Oversee external audit relationship: coordinate with external auditors on statutory and GAAP audit; manage audit findings and management letter items
- Produce monthly financial close: P&L, balance sheet, and cash flow statement within 10 business days of month-end

### Statutory Insurance Financial Management
- Manage insurance holding company financial requirements: state regulator financial oversight, holding company act filings
- Monitor **Risk-Based Capital (RBC) ratio**: target surplus above Company Action Level (200% CAL minimum; 300%+ operational target)
- Oversee Annual Statement preparation: Life/Health Annual Statement, all schedules and exhibits
- Manage state premium tax filings across all licensed states
- Govern investment portfolio under NAIC SAP investment limitations: admitted asset rules, bond quality ratings, concentration limits

### Budget, Planning & Forecasting
- Lead annual financial planning and budgeting process: P&L budget, capital budget, and IT/marketing investment plan
- Develop rolling financial forecast: quarterly updates with variance analysis vs. budget
- Build financial models for major investment decisions: TCO analysis, build vs. buy, new product launch business case
- Govern marketing budget: total marketing spend budget by channel (Digital vs. Print); CPA (Cost per Application) and CPL (Cost per Lead) financial targets
- Produce monthly **Financial Management Report** for executive team: actual vs. budget, forecast update, key driver commentary

### Marketing Investment Governance & ROI Analysis
- Define the financial framework for marketing investment decisions:
  - **Cost per Application (CPA)**: total marketing cost ÷ applications submitted; primary acquisition efficiency metric
  - **Cost per Issued Policy (CPIP)**: total marketing cost ÷ issued policies; accounts for underwriting fallout
  - **Marketing expense ratio**: total marketing spend ÷ total premiums written
  - **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) payback period**: CPIP ÷ monthly net premium income per policy
- Evaluate channel-level P&L: Digital vs. Print channel profitability accounting for response rate, conversion rate, policy size, and lapse rate differences
- Set annual marketing budget guardrails: by channel, by product, and by acquisition vehicle; provide go/no-go financial thresholds for campaign investment
- Partner with Marketing Forecasting Specialist to validate financial assumptions in volume and revenue projections

### Capital & Treasury Management
- Manage invested asset portfolio within NAIC SAP investment guidelines: asset allocation, duration matching to reserve liabilities
- Oversee reinsurance financial management: ceded premium accounting, reinsurance recoverables, treaty settlements
- Manage holding company cash liquidity: dividend capacity from the insurance subsidiary; parent company working capital
- Evaluate capital alternatives: retained earnings, capital contributions, reinsurance as a capital relief tool (with Chief Actuary)

### Technology & Operations Financial Governance
- Finance-govern IT and technology investments: technology budget approval thresholds, CapEx vs. OpEx treatment, multi-year contract financial commitments
- Evaluate SaaS contract economics: total cost of ownership, vendor lock-in cost, contract exit cost
- Govern AWS cloud spend: monthly cloud cost review; cost allocation by business unit; anomaly detection for unexpected spend
- Provide financial due diligence for major vendor contracts: evaluate financial terms, payment schedules, SLA penalty exposure

### Regulatory Financial Compliance
- Ensure compliance with New York Insurance Law financial requirements and NYDFS financial oversight
- Manage state market conduct examination financial information requests
- Oversee NAIC Financial Examiners review preparation
- Manage Sarbanes-Oxley equivalent internal controls over financial reporting (if applicable as NYL subsidiary)
- Coordinate with Lead Compliance Officer on financially-relevant consumer protection obligations (refund processing, claim payment timeliness)

## Key Financial Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Target |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| RBC Ratio | Statutory capital ÷ Required capital | ≥300% CAL |
| Marketing Expense Ratio | Total marketing spend ÷ Total premium | Managed to plan |
| CPA | Total marketing cost ÷ Applications | Channel-specific budget |
| CPIP | Total marketing cost ÷ Issued policies | Channel-specific budget |
| Lapse Rate | Policies lapsed in year ÷ Policies in force | Per pricing assumption |
| DAC Amortization Rate | GAAP DAC expense ÷ Gross premium | Per LDTI model |
| AWS Cost per Policy | Monthly cloud spend ÷ Active policies | Efficiency target |

## Collaboration Interfaces

- **Chief Actuary**: Reserve calculations, RBC management, reinsurance economics, experience study financial implications
- **Chief Marketing Officer**: Marketing budget governance, channel ROI analysis, campaign investment decisions
- **CIO / CTO**: Technology investment approvals, AWS cost management, vendor contract financial terms
- **Data Governance Lead**: Data quality in financial reporting datasets; lineage for audit support
- **Enterprise Data Architect**: Financial data platform requirements; Redshift access for financial reporting
- **Marketing Forecasting Specialist**: Validate financial assumptions in marketing volume forecasts
- **Lead Compliance Officer**: Financial penalties from regulatory actions; consumer refund and claim payment obligations
