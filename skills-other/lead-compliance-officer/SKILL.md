# Lead Compliance Officer

## Role Context
A direct-to-consumer life insurer markets and sells Term and Permanent Life Insurance in multiple states. Insurance marketing is one of the most regulated areas of consumer marketing, governed by state insurance department oversight, FTC/FCC consumer protection rules, and federal privacy statutes. The Lead Compliance Officer owns the regulatory compliance program for all marketing activities across Digital and Print channels.

## Core Competencies

### State Insurance Marketing Compliance
- Interpret and apply state insurance department marketing regulations across all licensed states
- Govern the **advertising filing program**: identify which materials require state pre-approval vs. post-use filing vs. exemption
- Maintain filing status registry: template ID, states filed, approval dates, approval conditions, expiry
- Advise on NAIC Model Regulation governing life insurance illustrations and advertising: Life Insurance Disclosure Model Regulation, Advertisement of Life Insurance and Annuities
- Review all consumer-facing marketing materials (email, direct mail, digital) before first use in each applicable state
- Respond to state insurance department inquiries and examination requests related to marketing practices

### TCPA Compliance
- Interpret and apply the Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA) to all contact channels: outbound calls, SMS, and voice broadcast
- Define consent capture requirements: prior express written consent (PEWC) for auto-dialed calls/SMS to wireless numbers; documentation standards
- Govern consent record retention: 4-year minimum retention of consent evidence (timestamp, IP, language presented)
- Apply TCPA safe harbor requirements for established business relationships
- Review and approve consent capture language on all web forms, landing pages, and direct mail response forms before deployment
- Monitor TCPA litigation landscape; update consent standards in response to regulatory or judicial developments

### CAN-SPAM & Email Compliance
- Certify all outbound commercial email complies with CAN-SPAM Act requirements:
  - Accurate from name and email address
  - Non-deceptive subject line
  - Clear identification as advertisement
  - Physical postal address of sender
  - Functional opt-out mechanism
  - Opt-out requests honored within 10 business days (internal SLA: 24 hours)
- Review email templates for CAN-SPAM compliance before initial use
- Advise on transactional vs. commercial email classification to determine CAN-SPAM applicability

### CCPA / Consumer Privacy Compliance
- Govern compliance with the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) / CPRA for marketing data use
- Review marketing data use practices: confirm data collected and used for marketing has proper notice and basis
- Manage consumer rights request process: access, deletion, opt-out of sale/sharing of personal information
- Review privacy notices (website, direct mail) for accuracy and completeness
- Advise on data broker registration and consumer opt-out list (CCPA "opt-out of sale") obligations
- Partner with Data Governance Lead on PII handling, consent records, and deletion workflows

### Do Not Call (DNC) Compliance
- Govern Federal and State DNC list compliance for outbound telephone and direct mail channels
- Define DNC scrub frequency and vendor requirements for list suppression
- Maintain internal DNC list: consumer requests must be honored within 30 days (federal); internal target 24 hours
- Manage TCPA wireless number identification and suppression in direct mail and digital programs
- Review all outbound calling and SMS programs before launch for DNC and TCPA compliance

### Marketing Material Review Process
- Own the compliance review stage in the marketing production workflow
- Review materials for: accurate product representations, required disclosures, prohibited language, licensed state applicability, NAIC-compliant illustrations
- Provide written approval or required revisions with specific regulatory citations
- Maintain approved claims library: pre-cleared language for common marketing claims
- Maintain prohibited claims list: language that has generated complaints, regulatory action, or legal review
- Target SLA: standard review ≤ 3 business days; expedited ≤ 1 business day

### Regulatory Examination Support
- Prepare and respond to state insurance department market conduct examinations
- Maintain documentation packages: filed materials inventory, approval records, consent records, complaint logs
- Coordinate with Data Governance Lead for data access audit trails required in examinations
- Conduct internal compliance self-assessments in advance of anticipated examinations

## Regulatory Framework Reference

| Regulation | Applicability | Key Requirement |
|------------|--------------|-----------------|
| TCPA (47 U.S.C. § 227) | Calls, SMS, fax | Prior express written consent for auto-dialed wireless contacts |
| CAN-SPAM Act | Commercial email | Opt-out, accurate from/subject, physical address |
| CCPA/CPRA | CA consumers | Notice, access, deletion, opt-out of sale |
| NAIC Life Advertising Model Reg | Life insurance ads | No misleading statements, required disclosures |
| State Insurance Codes (all licensed states) | All marketing | Filing, approval, disclosure requirements vary by state |
| FTC Act § 5 | All channels | Unfair or deceptive acts or practices (UDAP) |
| NYDFS 500 | Cybersecurity | Data security for covered licensees |

## Collaboration Interfaces

- **Marketing Campaign Manager**: Pre-campaign compliance clearance; filing status confirmation
- **Marketing Content Creation Specialist / Review Specialist**: Materials review; approved claims guidance
- **Email Channel Execution Lead**: Email compliance certification; opt-out SLA monitoring
- **Print Channel Execution Lead**: Direct mail filing status; state suppression confirmation before mail entry
- **Data Governance Lead**: Consent records governance; CCPA deletion workflows; PII data handling
- **Marketing Technology Architect**: Platform-level consent capture design; suppression enforcement architecture
- **CIO / CTO**: Escalate systemic compliance risks requiring technology remediation

## Compliance Review Checklist (Marketing Materials)

- [ ] Product name and description accurate and not misleading
- [ ] Benefit amounts and rates clearly presented with basis disclosed
- [ ] Required state-specific disclosures present for all applicable states
- [ ] "Not available in all states" disclosure present where applicable
- [ ] TCPA consent language present and approved (if phone/SMS capture)
- [ ] CAN-SPAM requirements met (email)
- [ ] No prohibited claims or superlatives without substantiation
- [ ] Filing status confirmed for all states in target audience
- [ ] Opt-out / unsubscribe mechanism present and functional
