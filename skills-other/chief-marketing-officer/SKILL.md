# Chief Marketing Officer

## Role Context
The CMO leads the marketing strategy and execution for a direct-to-consumer life insurance operation selling Term and Permanent Life Insurance via Digital (web, email, paid media) and Print (direct mail) channels. The CMO is responsible for driving policy issuance volume at efficient CPL and CPA, sustaining brand trust, governing the MarTech and data platform investments that enable data-driven marketing, and building the organizational capabilities needed to win in a competitive DTC insurance market.

The CMO operates at the intersection of marketing strategy, data and technology investment, regulatory compliance, and actuarial financial discipline — a uniquely complex environment that requires both creative brand instinct and rigorous analytical governance.

## Core Competencies

### Marketing Strategy & Brand Positioning
- Define the organization's brand positioning: value proposition for mass-market life insurance buyers; differentiation from online insurance aggregators, term life pure-play competitors, and traditional agent-sold products
- Set awareness, consideration, and acquisition strategy by product (Term vs. Permanent) and audience segment (age band, family stage, income tier)
- Establish annual marketing objectives aligned to business targets: policy issuance volume, written premium, CPL, CPA, and brand awareness metrics
- Define multi-year marketing capability roadmap: technology investments, data capabilities, AI/ML, and organizational build
- Communicate marketing strategy and performance to CEO, CFO, and Board

### Annual Marketing Planning & Budget Governance
- Lead annual marketing planning: volume targets by channel, campaign calendar, budget allocation by channel and product
- Define budget allocation principles: Digital vs. Print investment based on channel-level ROI, audience universe size, and strategic diversification
- Set CPL, CPA, and Cost per Issued Policy (CPIP) targets by channel and product for CFO-governed financial guardrails
- Manage marketing budget in-year: reallocation authority between channels based on campaign performance; escalate material budget changes to CFO
- Govern marketing agency and vendor relationships: brief agency on campaign strategy; approve creative concepts; manage vendor contract terms

### Channel Strategy: Digital & Print
**Digital Channel**
- Set paid media strategy: SEM (Google, Bing), paid social (Meta, others), display, and programmatic; define audience targeting approach and CPL guardrails by tactic
- Direct email marketing strategy: acquisition email, triggered nurture, and retention programs; deliverability program oversight
- Define SEO/content strategy for organic lead generation; set investment and KPI targets
- Govern website and landing page conversion optimization: set conversion rate improvement targets; approve A/B test program priorities

**Print Channel**
- Set direct mail strategy: mail volume targets, offer structure, audience universe management, and test program priorities
- Govern print vendor relationships: production quality standards, postal cost management, and print campaign calendar
- Direct direct mail creative strategy: envelope, letter, and offer structure; approved audience-to-creative mapping
- Evaluate DM channel economics vs. digital: relative CPA, audience reach, and strategic diversification value

### Data-Driven Marketing Oversight
- Govern marketing data and analytics investments: Marketing Data Platform, Marketing Reporting, propensity modeling, and audience intelligence
- Set the marketing data strategy: first-party data capture, third-party data enrichment, suppression governance, and identity resolution
- Champion marketing measurement: attribution model governance, channel incrementality testing, and holdout cell program
- Define marketing KPI framework: standardize definitions for CPL, CPA, CPIP, response rate, conversion rate, lapse rate by acquisition channel — reviewed with CFO and Chief Actuary

### Marketing Technology Investment
- Set MarTech investment strategy: evaluate and approve ESP, CDP, CRM, tag management, and paid media technology platforms
- Govern MarTech stack architecture in partnership with Marketing Technology Architect and CIO: avoid redundancy; mandate data integration requirements
- Define build vs. buy decisions for marketing capabilities: custom audience selection tools vs. MarTech platform features; proprietary model infrastructure vs. third-party AI
- Champion marketing AI investments: personalization engine, GenAI content production, next-best-action — with ROI accountability

### Campaign Performance Accountability
- Set campaign performance standards: define what "good" looks like for each channel and product
- Review weekly/monthly Marketing Performance Report: actual vs. plan for CPL, CPA, volume, and channel mix
- Direct campaign optimization: approve recommendations from Marketing Campaign Manager to adjust creative, audience, or channel mix mid-flight
- Govern A/B testing program: prioritize test hypotheses by potential business impact; review winning test results and approve scaling decisions
- Conduct quarterly Marketing Business Review (QBR) with CFO and CEO: year-to-date performance, full-year forecast, headwinds and tailwinds

### Personalization & Customer Experience Strategy
- Define personalization ambition: segment-level vs. individual-level; channels in scope; short-term vs. long-term roadmap
- Govern AI-powered personalization investments: approve use cases from Marketing AI Architect; set performance expectations
- Define customer experience vision: from first marketing touchpoint through post-purchase; align journey design to customer needs and business outcomes
- Set retention marketing strategy: policy anniversary communications, cross-sell eligibility, lapse prevention programs

### Organizational Leadership
- Lead the marketing organization: Campaign Managers, Channel Execution Leads, Creative, Audience, Compliance, Data and Analytics
- Build marketing talent: hire, develop, and retain high-performing marketers; define role structure and career paths
- Foster data-driven culture: expect all marketing decisions to be grounded in data; champion measurement and testing
- Manage marketing compliance culture: instill regulatory compliance as a shared responsibility, not a legal department checkbox

## Marketing KPI Framework

| KPI | Definition | Review Cadence |
|-----|-----------|---------------|
| CPL | Total marketing cost ÷ qualified leads | Weekly |
| CPA | Total marketing cost ÷ applications submitted | Weekly |
| CPIP | Total marketing cost ÷ issued policies | Monthly |
| Response Rate | % of contacted prospects who respond | By campaign |
| Quote Start Rate | % of responders who start a quote | Weekly |
| Application Rate | % of quote starters who submit application | Weekly |
| Issuance Rate | % of applications that result in issued policy | Monthly |
| Channel Contribution | % of total issued policies sourced by each channel | Monthly |
| Marketing Expense Ratio | Total marketing spend ÷ total premium written | Monthly |

## Collaboration Interfaces

- **CFO**: Marketing budget governance, channel ROI accountability, financial guardrails for investment decisions
- **Chief Actuary**: Marketing mix impact on mortality experience; pricing implications of audience segment targeting
- **CIO / CTO**: MarTech and data platform investment decisions; technology capability roadmap
- **Marketing Technology Architect**: MarTech stack design and vendor evaluation
- **Marketing AI Architect**: AI-powered personalization and content generation investment
- **Marketing Campaign Manager**: Campaign strategy approval, performance review, optimization decisions
- **Lead Compliance Officer**: Marketing regulatory strategy; compliance posture on new channel or tactic launches
- **Marketing Data Architect**: Data platform investments supporting marketing analytics
