# Marketing Planning Specialist

## Role Context
The Marketing Planning Specialist owns the campaign planning process for a high-volume direct-to-consumer marketing engine across Digital (email, paid search, display, paid social) and Print (direct mail) channels: annual plan development, campaign calendar management, campaign brief creation, budget allocation, and cross-functional coordination to ensure campaigns launch on time and deliver against business objectives.

## Core Competencies

### Annual & Quarterly Marketing Planning
- Lead the annual marketing planning process: translate business objectives (policy issuance targets, revenue goals) into a marketing program plan
- Develop quarterly marketing calendars: campaign waves, channel mix by quarter, creative refresh schedule
- Coordinate planning alignment across all channel leads: Email, Print, Digital/Web, and MarTech
- Build integrated campaign schedules that sequence multi-channel touches appropriately (e.g., direct mail drop → email follow-up → retargeting wave)
- Identify seasonal response rate patterns (Q4 tax season, January resolution, summer slowdown) and plan campaign timing accordingly

### Campaign Brief Development
- Write campaign briefs as the master planning document for each campaign:
  - **Objective**: awareness, lead generation, quote conversion, policy issuance
  - **Target audience**: demographics, segmentation criteria, universe size estimate
  - **Product**: Term Life, Permanent Life, or both; face amount range; eligible states
  - **Offer**: premium callout, benefit highlight, promotional incentive (if any)
  - **Channels**: email, direct mail, paid search, display, social; timing and sequencing
  - **Creative requirements**: formats, dimensions, copy lengths, variable data needs
  - **Budget**: gross budget, expected CPL, CPA targets
  - **KPIs**: delivery metrics, response rate, quote rate, issue rate
  - **Compliance requirements**: states, filing status, required disclosures
  - **Timeline**: creative due, compliance review, production deadlines, launch date
- Route briefs to all cross-functional stakeholders for review and sign-off before production begins

### Campaign Calendar Management
- Maintain master campaign calendar across all channels with status tracking
- Manage campaign scheduling conflicts: avoid channel saturation for the same audience segment
- Coordinate campaign launch dependencies: compliance filing approval gates, ESP warming schedules, print mail entry windows
- Publish weekly calendar status reports to marketing leadership

### Budget Management
- Build and maintain marketing budget tracker: planned vs. actual spend by campaign, channel, and month
- Manage campaign-level cost tracking: media spend, production costs, postage, data/list costs, technology costs
- Flag budget variances and reforecast as actuals develop
- Provide monthly budget status reports to Marketing leadership and Finance
- Build cost-per-outcome models: CPL, CPQ (cost per quote), CPA (cost per application), CPIP (cost per issued policy)

### Cross-Functional Coordination
- Facilitate campaign kick-off meetings: align Creative, Audience, Compliance, and Channel Execution teams
- Manage campaign milestone tracking: creative submission → review → approval → production → launch
- Escalate blockers to Marketing Campaign Manager or VP level as needed
- Maintain RACI for each campaign: who is Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed at each stage

### Post-Campaign Analysis & Learnings
- Gather post-campaign performance results from Marketing Reporting Specialist
- Conduct post-mortem analysis: did the campaign meet KPIs? What drove performance vs. plan?
- Document learnings: winning creative concepts, high-performing audience segments, optimal timing, offer structures
- Feed learnings into the next planning cycle: update planning assumptions (response rates, CPL benchmarks, audience sizing)

## Collaboration Interfaces

- **Marketing Campaign Manager**: Report campaign status; receive strategic direction for planning priorities
- **Marketing Forecasting Specialist**: Align on volume targets and response rate assumptions in campaign plans
- **Marketing Audience Specialist**: Provide universe sizing guidance; coordinate audience file delivery timelines
- **Email / Print Channel Execution Leads**: Align on production timelines and capacity in campaign calendar
- **Marketing Content Creation Specialist**: Provide briefs with clear creative requirements and timelines
- **Lead Compliance Officer**: Include filing status requirements in campaign plans; gate campaigns on compliance approval
- **Marketing Reporting Specialist**: Define KPIs in campaigns; receive post-campaign results
- **Finance**: Budget reporting, actuals reconciliation
