# Marketing Content Creation Specialist

## Role Context
The Marketing Content Creation Specialist writes consumer-facing marketing copy for direct-to-consumer life insurance across digital (email, paid search, display, social) and print (direct mail) channels. Content must convert skeptical, price-sensitive consumers who may have limited familiarity with life insurance. All content is subject to state insurance marketing filing requirements and compliance review before deployment.

## Core Competencies

### Direct-to-Consumer Life Insurance Copywriting
- Write benefit-focused copy that translates complex insurance product features into clear consumer value
- Apply plain language principles: 6th–8th grade reading level for primary body copy; avoid jargon
- Lead with emotional resonance (family protection, peace of mind, affordability) supported by rational proof points (price, simplicity, no medical exam for simplified issue)
- Craft urgency and relevance without deceptive tactics prohibited by insurance marketing regulations
- Write for diverse consumer demographics: seniors (50–75), parents with young families, and underinsured working adults

### Email Copy
- Write subject lines: 35–50 character optimal range; preheader copy extending the subject message
- Develop email body copy hierarchy: hook → benefit statement → offer → CTA
- Write A/B test variants for subject lines, headlines, and CTAs with hypothesis-driven rationale
- Adapt tone across lifecycle stages: acquisition (benefit-lead), nurture (educational), re-engagement (value reminder)
- Comply with CAN-SPAM: no deceptive subject lines; from name consistent with brand

### Direct Mail Copy
- Write outer envelope teaser copy driving open rates
- Develop letter copy: personalized salutation, AIDA structure (Attention → Interest → Desire → Action)
- Write buckslip and lift note copy for package inserts
- Compose BRE (Business Reply Envelope) and response form copy
- Apply variable data copy rules: personalized name blocks, offer amounts by age/state, benefit amount call-outs
- Ensure all state-required disclosures are incorporated per Compliance guidance without disrupting copy flow

### Landing Page & Digital Copy
- Write SEO-optimized page titles, H1s, and meta descriptions aligned to keyword strategy
- Develop landing page copy with singular CTA focus: above-fold headline, benefit bullets, social proof, CTA
- Write product description copy: feature/benefit format for term and permanent life products
- Create FAQ copy addressing top consumer objections: cost, medical exams, coverage amounts, claims process
- Write ad copy for paid search (RSA headlines/descriptions within character limits) and display (headline + description)

### Content Briefs & Messaging Frameworks
- Develop campaign content briefs: objective, audience, offer, key messages, channel, word count, compliance constraints
- Maintain brand messaging framework: brand voice, tone guidelines, approved claims, product value propositions
- Document approved and prohibited claim language aligned with state insurance filing requirements
- Create message maps by product (term vs. perm) and audience segment (age band, life stage)

### Regulatory & Compliance Awareness
- Write content that anticipates compliance review: avoid absolute claims ("best," "guaranteed" outside approved usage), unsubstantiated superlatives, or misleading comparisons
- Incorporate required disclosures naturally into copy flow: licensing disclosure, rate disclaimer, not available in all states
- Flag content elements requiring state filing review before routing to Marketing Content Review Specialist
- Understand which copy elements are filing-sensitive vs. non-filing (general educational content)

## Content Standards

| Element | Standard |
|---------|----------|
| Email subject line | 35–50 characters; no ALL CAPS deception; A/B tested |
| Email body (acquisition) | 150–300 words; single primary CTA |
| Direct mail letter | 1–2 pages; personalized; AIDA structure |
| Landing page body | 200–400 words above fold; benefit bullets; one CTA |
| Paid search headline | ≤ 30 characters; keyword-relevant |
| Paid search description | ≤ 90 characters; benefit-focused |
| Reading level | Flesch-Kincaid Grade 6–8 for primary copy |

## Brand Voice & Tone

- **Empathetic**: We understand protecting family is personal and important
- **Straightforward**: Plain language, clear offers, no fine print surprises
- **Trustworthy**: Backed by the parent company's heritage of financial strength
- **Accessible**: Life insurance doesn't have to be complicated or expensive
- **Respectful**: Never exploit fear; lead with empowerment and peace of mind

## Prohibited Content (Compliance Boundaries)

- Unsubstantiated price superiority claims without disclosed basis
- "Guaranteed approval" language without specifying the underwriting basis
- Health claims or medical advice
- Misleading comparisons to competitor products by name
- Copy implying regulatory endorsement of the product
- "Free" offers that have conditions not clearly disclosed

## Collaboration Interfaces

- **Marketing Campaign Manager**: Receive campaign briefs; align content to campaign objectives and timelines
- **Marketing Creative Strategist**: Align copy to visual concept and campaign creative direction
- **Marketing Content Review Specialist**: Submit copy for compliance and brand review before production
- **Lead Compliance Officer**: Escalate filing-sensitive language questions; receive approved claim library
- **Marketing Content Manager**: Deliver final approved copy assets to CMS/DAM for deployment
- **Email Channel Execution Lead**: Provide final email copy ready for ESP build
- **Print Channel Execution Lead**: Provide final direct mail copy with variable data specifications

4. Paid search ad copy set (RSA headlines and descriptions)
5. Campaign messaging framework / content brief for a new product launch

## AI Collaborative Writing

As of 2026, content creation specialists are expected to work with AI content generation tools as collaborators — not replacements. The skills are:

### Directing AI-Assisted Content Production
- Write effective content briefs that produce usable AI output: specify audience, reading level, tone, compliance constraints, required claims, prohibited language, and CTA structure in the brief that feeds AI tools
- Evaluate AI-generated copy against brand voice, insurance regulatory standards, and campaign brief requirements — AI does not know the compliance library; the specialist applies that knowledge to review
- Identify AI failure modes specific to insurance copy: hallucinated product features, overstated guarantees, omitted required disclosures, state-specific eligibility errors, reading-level drift upward to jargon
- Use AI for appropriate tasks: generating multiple headline variants for A/B test consideration, adapting a single message across formats (email to SMS to display), initial draft of FAQ copy
- Protect judgment-intensive tasks from pure AI execution: emotional resonance calibration, compliance-sensitive claim framing, narrative brand voice consistency across a long-form campaign
