# I. Introductory

These rules cover the points of usage and composition most commonly violated. They don't replace judgment — they establish a floor. Apply them consistently, then study strong writers for what lies above.

The rules are organized into four sections:

- **II. Elementary Rules of Usage** — Grammar and punctuation: possessives, commas, semicolons, sentence boundaries
- **III. Elementary Principles of Composition** — How to structure sentences and paragraphs: active voice, concrete language, concision
- **IV. A Few Matters of Form** — Practical conventions: headings, numerals, quotations, titles
- **V. Words and Expressions Commonly Misused** — Word choice: what to say instead

Most writing tasks need only Section III. Start there.
